## Consent Collection for Registration

- Marketing consent must be a SEPARATE checkbox from registration. Never bundled.
- No pre-ticked boxes. Hard GDPR requirement (Article 7, Recital 32).
- Sponsor data sharing needs EXPLICIT consent PER SPONSOR. Not a blanket "share with partners."
- Record timestamp, IP, and exact wording of each consent given.
- Withdrawal must be as easy as giving consent (unsubscribe link in every email).

**Consent wording — marketing:**
"I agree to receive marketing communications about future events from [Organization]. I can unsubscribe at any time."

**Consent wording — sponsor data sharing:**
"I agree that my registration details (name, email, company, job title) may be shared with the following event sponsors: [list sponsors]. Each sponsor will contact me independently and I can opt out from each."

## Data Minimization for Forms

Collect only what the event actually needs. More fields = lower conversion AND higher compliance risk.

**Minimum required:** First name, last name, email, ticket type
**Reasonable additions:** Company, job title, country
**Only if genuinely needed:** Phone (if SMS updates planned), dietary requirements (if catering — delete 30 days post-event), accessibility needs (if venue accommodations needed)
**Never require for basic registration:** Company revenue, social handles, date of birth

## Privacy Notice on Registration

Link prominently from the registration form BEFORE data submission. Must include:
1. Data controller name and contact
2. What data is collected and why
3. Who receives the data (sponsors, platform — be explicit)
4. Retention period
5. Attendee rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability)
6. Right to withdraw consent
7. Supervisory authority contact

## Double Opt-In (Germany)

German case law effectively requires double opt-in for email marketing. After the consent checkbox is ticked, send a confirmation email with a verification link. Only add to marketing list after click. This applies to events in Germany or targeting German attendees.

## Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Registration data (name, email, company) | 12 months post-event | Follow-up and future invitations |
| Financial records (invoices, payments) | 7-10 years per tax law | Legal obligation |
| Marketing consent records | As long as active + 3 years | Proof of consent |
| Dietary / accessibility data | 30 days post-event | No longer needed |
| Badge scan data shared with sponsors | 6 months max | Sponsor follow-up cycle |

## Registration Compliance Checklist

- [ ] Privacy notice linked from registration form
- [ ] Marketing consent checkbox is separate and unchecked by default
- [ ] Sponsor data sharing disclosed with sponsor names listed
- [ ] Double opt-in configured (German events)
- [ ] Registration platform has signed Data Processing Agreement
- [ ] International data transfer assessment done (if platform is US-based)
- [ ] Data retention schedule defined and documented
- [ ] Process for handling data deletion requests documented
- [ ] Staff briefed on data handling (who accesses what)
- [ ] Cookie consent on event website (TTDSG for Germany)
