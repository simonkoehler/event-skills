## ROI Calculation Methods

### Pipeline ROI (most common for B2B)

```
Pipeline ROI = (Pipeline generated from event ÷ Total event cost) × 100
```

Example: 500K pipeline from a 100K event = 500% pipeline ROI. Industry benchmark: 3-5x pipeline ROI is considered strong for B2B events.

### Revenue ROI (hardest to attribute, most valued by leadership)

```
Revenue ROI = (Revenue closed from event-sourced pipeline ÷ Total event cost) × 100
```

Requires CRM attribution tracking. Typical close rate on event-sourced leads: 10-20% for conferences, 5-10% for trade shows. Attribution window: 90-180 days post-event.

### Cost per Lead (CPL)

```
Cost per Lead = Total event cost ÷ Qualified leads generated
```

B2B benchmarks (forward-looking projections):

| Event Type | Average CPL | Good CPL | Excellent CPL |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conference | 40-100% of per-attendee cost | 20-40% of per-attendee cost | Under 20% of per-attendee cost |
| Trade show | 60-160% of per-attendee cost | 30-60% of per-attendee cost | Under 30% of per-attendee cost |

Compare against other channels (digital ads typically 15-50% of event CPL, content marketing 25-75% of event CPL). These benchmarks are identical to the post-event measurement benchmarks in event-analytics — use the same ranges for projection and measurement.

### Cost per Meeting

```
Cost per Meeting = Total event cost ÷ Meetings booked at/from event
```

B2B benchmark: 100-300% of per-attendee cost per meeting. Note: only 18% of trade show leads are ever followed up, so the real denominator is often much smaller than total scans.

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)

Not a financial ROI but critical for recurring events:

| NPS Range | Rating | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 70+ | Excellent | Maintain, consider premium pricing |
| 50-70 | Good | Identify improvement areas |
| 30-50 | Average | Significant changes needed |
| Below 30 | Poor | Major redesign required |

## Break-Even Formulas

### Ticket-Revenue Break-Even

```
Break-even registrations = (Total Fixed Costs + (Variable Cost × Attendance) − Sponsorship) ÷ Average Ticket Price
```

Simplified: if fixed costs = 50% of budget, variable cost per head = 15% of ticket price, sponsorship covers 30% of fixed costs, solve for N attendees at your ticket price.

### Sponsor-Funded Break-Even

For events where sponsors cover most costs:
```
Required sponsorship = Total Expenses − (Ticket Revenue + Other Income)
```

## Cost Justification Framework

When building a business case for event investment, structure the argument as:

1. **Investment ask:** Total event budget + staff time allocation
2. **Expected returns:** Pipeline target, leads target, meetings target, brand impressions
3. **Benchmark comparison:** Cost per lead vs. other channels, historical event ROI
4. **Risk mitigation:** Contingency budget, cancellation insurance, scope flex points
5. **Success criteria:** Specific KPIs with targets, measurement methodology, reporting timeline
6. **Alternative cost:** What would it cost to generate equivalent pipeline through other channels?

**Key argument:** Events generate higher-quality leads than digital channels. Event-sourced leads close at 2-3x the rate of digitally-sourced leads and have 25-30% higher average deal size.
