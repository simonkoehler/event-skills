## Lawful Basis for Exhibitor Data Processing

| Processing Activity | Lawful Basis | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Badge scanning at your booth | Consent (implied by scan action + signage) | Attendee must be informed before scanning |
| Adding scanned leads to your CRM | Legitimate interest | Must be related to the interaction at the booth |
| Sending follow-up emails to scanned leads | Legitimate interest (first email) or consent (ongoing) | First email can reference the booth visit; marketing sequences need consent |
| Pre-show outreach to target accounts | Legitimate interest or consent | Depends on data source — see Pre-Show Outreach section |
| Sharing lead data with your sales team | Internal processing | No separate basis needed if same controller |
| Using organizer-provided attendee lists | Consent (collected by organizer) | Verify organizer collected valid consent before using |
| Post-show reporting with attendee-level data | Legitimate interest | Anonymize where possible — use aggregated metrics |

## Lead Capture and Badge Scanning

**Before the show:**
- Confirm with organizer: what data does a badge scan capture? (Typically: name, email, company, job title)
- Confirm scanning terms: does the attendee consent to sponsor scanning at registration? How?
- Configure your scanning app to capture only necessary fields
- Prepare booth signage: "Scanning your badge shares your contact details with [Your Company Name]"

**During the show:**
- Display GDPR signage at booth entrance — attendees must see it before being scanned
- Attendees can decline scanning — do not condition swag, content, or raffle entries on badge scan
- If running a raffle or giveaway: separate consent for marketing use ("Enter our raffle" ≠ "Subscribe to our newsletter")
- Business card collection: same rules apply — verbal consent for follow-up is minimum
- Qualification notes in scanning app: stick to professional context (need, timeline, product interest) — no personal observations

**Signage template:**
"By having your badge scanned at the [Company Name] booth, you agree that we may contact you regarding [product/service area]. Your data (name, email, company, job title) will be stored in our CRM and retained for a maximum of 6 months. You can request deletion at any time by emailing {{privacy_email}}."

## Pre-Show Outreach

**Data sourced from your own CRM (existing contacts):**
- Legitimate interest for event-related outreach to existing business contacts
- Include opt-out in every email
- Reference the existing relationship: "As a [relationship context], we wanted to let you know..."

**Data sourced from the event organizer (attendee list):**
- Verify that the organizer collected explicit consent for sponsor communications
- Request documentation of consent mechanism
- If consent is unclear: do NOT use for cold outreach — only for awareness (e.g., "visit us at booth 42" in event communications, not your own email campaign)

**Data sourced from LinkedIn or public sources:**
- LinkedIn InMail: governed by LinkedIn terms, not GDPR (platform-to-platform)
- Direct email to publicly listed business contacts: legitimate interest may apply, but include opt-out and state your lawful basis
- Purchased contact lists: high GDPR risk — avoid for EU contacts unless seller can prove consent

## CRM and Follow-Up Compliance

**Post-show email sequence GDPR rules:**
- A-leads (personal follow-up): legitimate interest — reference the specific booth conversation
- B-leads (template follow-up): legitimate interest — reference the event and interaction
- C-leads (nurture sequence): requires consent — first email can be legitimate interest, but ongoing marketing needs opt-in
- All follow-up emails must include: sender identity, why they are receiving the email, unsubscribe mechanism
- German recipients: double opt-in required before adding to marketing automation (UWG requirement)

**CRM tagging:**
- Tag leads with event source: "[Event Name] [Year]"
- Do not merge event lead data with purchased list data
- Set automatic review/deletion flag at 6-month mark for leads that don't convert to active opportunities

## Assessing the Organizer's Compliance

Before participating, check that the event organizer handles GDPR properly — their failures can affect your data processing.

**Questions to ask the organizer:**
1. How is attendee consent collected for sponsor communications?
2. What data is included in badge scans?
3. Is a DPA required between organizer and exhibitor?
4. What is the attendee opt-out rate for sponsor data sharing? (Typical: 40-60% opt in)
5. Will the organizer provide consent documentation?

**Red flags:**
- Organizer cannot explain consent mechanism
- "All attendees automatically consent to sponsor contact" (this is not valid GDPR consent)
- No DPA offered or required
- Attendee list provided without proof of consent

## Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Scanned leads (not converted) | 6 months post-event | Delete or request fresh consent |
| Scanned leads (active opportunity) | Duration of business relationship | Standard CRM retention applies |
| Pre-show outreach records | 6 months post-event | Delete if no response |
| Organizer-provided attendee lists | 6 months post-event | Delete per organizer agreement |
| Booth visitor analytics (aggregated) | Unlimited | No personal data |
| Post-show report (attendee-level) | 3 months post-event | Anonymize after internal review |

## Exhibition GDPR Checklist

**Pre-Show (T-4 weeks):**
- [ ] Organizer's consent mechanism verified
- [ ] DPA signed with event organizer (if required)
- [ ] Booth GDPR signage prepared
- [ ] Scanning app configured with minimum-necessary fields
- [ ] Follow-up email templates include sender ID, reason, and unsubscribe
- [ ] CRM campaign tag created with auto-review flag at 6 months

**During Show:**
- [ ] GDPR signage displayed at booth before first scan
- [ ] Attendees not conditioned on scanning for giveaways or content
- [ ] Business cards collected with verbal consent noted
- [ ] Raffle entries handled separately from marketing consent

**Post-Show (within 48h):**
- [ ] Leads imported to CRM with event source tag
- [ ] Follow-up emails reference specific booth interaction
- [ ] German leads flagged for double opt-in before marketing automation
- [ ] Non-responsive pre-show contacts flagged for deletion

**Post-Show (T+6 months):**
- [ ] Non-converted leads reviewed and deleted
- [ ] Organizer-provided lists deleted
- [ ] Post-show reports anonymized
