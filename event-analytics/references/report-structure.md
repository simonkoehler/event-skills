## Executive Report

Maximum 2 pages. Leadership wants: did we hit our goals, what did it cost, what should we do next.

**Structure:**

1. **Hero metric** — the single most important number, displayed prominently. Example: "{{currency}}1.2M pipeline generated at 5.4x ROI"
2. **Objectives vs. Results** — table format with RAG status

| Objective | Target | Actual | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Registrations | 500 | 450 | 🟡 90% |
| Qualified leads | 150 | 178 | 🟢 119% |
| Pipeline (90-day) | {{currency}}800K | {{currency}}1.2M | 🟢 150% |
| NPS | 50+ | 62 | 🟢 |
| Cost per attendee | <{{currency}}400 | {{currency}}380 | 🟢 |

3. **Financial summary** — total cost, total revenue, net result, cost per attendee
4. **Top 3 wins** — specific, not generic
5. **Top 3 improvements** — honest, with proposed solutions
6. **Recommendation** — continue / scale / modify / discontinue + next steps with owners

## Sponsor Report

One per sponsor, customized to their tier and deliverables.

**Structure:**

1. **Event overview** — attendee count, demographics, satisfaction scores
2. **Your deliverables fulfilled** — checklist with proof (screenshots of logo placement, social post links, email inclusion)
3. **Your metrics:**
   - Logo impressions (website traffic × placement, email sends × open rate)
   - Social mentions and reach (tagged posts, hashtag volume)
   - Booth traffic / leads collected (if applicable)
   - Session attendance (if speaking slot was part of package)
4. **Overall event performance** — NPS, attendance rate, engagement highlights
5. **Renewal offer** — early commitment discount, tier upgrade option

## Internal Report

Comprehensive 12-section report for the planning team. No polish needed — this is operational.

1. **Executive summary** — 2-3 sentences + hero metric
2. **Event overview** — date, venue, format, theme, team
3. **Goals vs. actuals** — full KPI table with RAG and commentary
4. **Attendance analysis** — registered vs. attended, no-show rate, demographics breakdown, registration source channels
5. **Session performance** — per-session attendance, ratings (if collected), top 3 and bottom 3 sessions
6. **Sponsor/exhibitor metrics** — leads per sponsor, booth traffic, satisfaction, renewal intent
7. **Financial summary** — budget vs. actuals by category, variance analysis, cost per attendee
8. **Marketing performance** — registrations by channel, email metrics, social reach, paid ad performance, cost per registration by channel
9. **Feedback and sentiment** — NPS score, satisfaction ratings, key verbatim quotes (positive and negative), top improvement requests
10. **What worked** — specific examples with evidence
11. **What didn't work** — specific examples with honest assessment, not blame
12. **Recommendations** — numbered action items, each with owner, deadline, and connection to next event planning

## Debrief Meeting Agenda

60-90 minutes. Schedule within 2 weeks of event while memory is fresh.

| Time | Topic | Lead | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:10 | Review objectives vs. results | Event Lead | Share report in advance |
| 0:10-0:20 | What exceeded expectations? | All | Round-table, each person names one thing |
| 0:20-0:30 | What fell short? | All | Honest, no blame, focus on systemic issues |
| 0:30-0:40 | What would we do differently with same budget? | All | Prioritize — top 3 changes only |
| 0:40-0:50 | Vendor assessment | Logistics Lead | Keep / renegotiate / replace for each vendor |
| 0:50-1:00 | Attendee feedback highlights | Analytics Lead | Survey data + verbatim quotes |
| 1:00-1:10 | Decisions for next edition | Event Lead | Date, format, scale, theme direction |
| 1:10-1:20 | Action items | Event Lead | Assign owner and deadline for each item |

**Action item rule:** Every lesson learned must map to a specific change in the next edition's event brief, timeline, or checklist. If a lesson doesn't lead to an action, it's an observation, not a lesson.
