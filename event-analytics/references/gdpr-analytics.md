# GDPR for Event Analytics

Load this reference when analyzing data from EU/EEA events.

## Legal Basis

- **Survey data:** Consent (obtained at survey start via clear opt-in)
- **Attendance tracking:** Legitimate interest (documented in privacy notice provided at registration)
- **Session ratings:** Anonymized — no personal data required
- **CRM attribution:** Consent obtained at registration (separate checkbox for data processing beyond event administration)

## Anonymization Rules

- **NPS scores and ratings:** Report only in aggregate (minimum group size: 10 respondents per segment)
- **Verbatim feedback:** Strip names, companies, and identifying details before including in reports
- **Lead attribution:** Pseudonymize during analysis. Delete individual-level data after final report.
- **Session attendance:** Report as counts and percentages, not individual attendee lists

## Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention | After Expiry |
|---|---|---|
| Survey raw data (with personal identifiers) | 12 months after event | Delete or fully anonymize |
| Aggregated reports (no personal data) | Unlimited | No action needed |
| Individual attendee analytics | 6 months after event | Delete |
| CRM campaign tags | Follow CRM retention policy | Per company policy |

## Reporting Safeguards

- Never include individual attendee names in analytics reports shared with sponsors
- Sponsor reports use aggregated metrics only (booth traffic count, lead count) — not individual attendee data
- If sharing attendee lists with sponsors, confirm explicit consent was collected at registration
- Executive reports: use only aggregated, non-identifiable data
