# GDPR for Sponsorship

Load this reference when designing sponsorship packages that involve attendee data sharing, or when managing exhibitor lead capture at EU/DACH events.

## Sponsor Data Sharing Consent

Sharing attendee data with sponsors requires EXPLICIT, SEPARATE consent from each attendee. A blanket "we share your data with event partners" is not GDPR-compliant.

**Rules:**
- List each sponsor BY NAME in the consent request
- Consent must be a separate checkbox from event registration consent
- Attendees must be able to consent to some sponsors but not others
- Never pre-tick sponsor sharing checkboxes
- Document what data is shared (name, email, company, job title — nothing beyond this without additional consent)

**Consent wording example:**
"I agree that my registration details (name, email, company, job title) may be shared with [Sponsor Name] for follow-up communication about their products and services. I can opt out at any time by contacting [email]."

## Lead Scanning at Booths

Badge scanning / QR code scanning at exhibitor booths is a separate data processing activity. Each exhibitor needs their own lawful basis.

- Attendees must be informed that having their badge scanned shares their data with that specific exhibitor
- Signage at booth: "Scanning your badge shares your contact details with [Exhibitor Name]"
- Attendees can decline to be scanned — exhibitors cannot make scanning a condition for swag or content
- Data from badge scans limited to: name, email, company, job title
- Exhibitors must sign a Data Processing Agreement with the event organizer

## Attendee List Sharing Rules

| Sponsor Tier | Data Access | Consent Required |
|---|---|---|
| Platinum (full list) | Name, email, company, job title of all attendees | Explicit opt-in from each attendee at registration |
| Gold/Silver (booth leads) | Only data from people who visited their booth / were scanned | Implied by badge scan action + signage |
| Bronze (no data) | No attendee data | None |

The "full attendee list" benefit in Platinum packages only works if attendees have consented. If consent rate is low (typical: 40-60% opt in), communicate realistic numbers to sponsors upfront. Don't promise what consent rates won't deliver.

## Retention and Deletion

- Sponsor-shared data: maximum 6 months retention
- Badge scan data: maximum 6 months retention
- Sponsors must confirm deletion after retention period (include in sponsor agreement)
- Event organizer retains proof of consent for 3 years after consent withdrawal

## Sponsor Agreement GDPR Clause

Include in every sponsor contract:
"Sponsor acknowledges that attendee data shared under this agreement is subject to GDPR. Sponsor agrees to: (a) use the data only for purposes disclosed to attendees at consent, (b) delete data within 6 months of receipt unless the attendee has given separate direct consent to Sponsor, (c) respond to data subject access requests within 30 days, (d) notify Organizer within 72 hours of any data breach affecting shared attendee data."
