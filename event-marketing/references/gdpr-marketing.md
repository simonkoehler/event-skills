# GDPR for Event Marketing

Load this reference when planning marketing campaigns for EU/EEA events.

## Email Marketing

- Only send to recipients with valid marketing consent
- Double opt-in required for German recipients (UWG — Gesetz gegen den unlauteren Wettbewerb)
- Every email must include: identifiable sender, legal notice (Impressum), and one-click unsubscribe link
- Re-engagement campaigns to lapsed contacts: permissible only if original consent covers it
- Past attendee emails: check if event registration consent covers marketing for future events — if not, re-consent needed

## Paid Advertising and Retargeting

- Website tracking pixels (Meta, LinkedIn, Google) require cookie consent (ePrivacy Directive)
- Custom Audiences from event attendee lists: only upload if attendees consented to marketing use of their data
- Retargeting campaigns: cookie consent must be obtained before placing tracking cookies
- Lookalike audiences built from consented lists are generally permissible (no personal data leaves the platform)

## Lead Generation

- Registration forms: marketing consent must be a separate, unchecked checkbox — never bundled with event registration consent
- Attendee lists: never share with sponsors without explicit, specific consent from each attendee
- Co-marketing with partners: each party needs independent consent or a clear data processing agreement

## Documentation

- Maintain a record of consent for all marketing communications
- Document the legal basis for each marketing channel in the campaign brief
- For cross-border campaigns (e.g., DACH + UK), note that UK GDPR applies separately post-Brexit
