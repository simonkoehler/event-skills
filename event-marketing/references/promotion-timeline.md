## Standard 12-Week Promotion Timeline

| Week | Phase | Activities | Registration Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-2 | Soft Launch | Website live, save-the-date email, first social posts, early-bird opens | 5-10% of target |
| 3-4 | Early Bird Push | Email campaign #2, LinkedIn ads launch, speaker announcement #1, partner outreach | 15-25% |
| 5-6 | Content Ramp | Blog posts, speaker spotlights (weekly), email #3, social proof posts | 30-40% |
| 7-8 | Agenda Release | Full agenda published, email #4 (agenda), session teasers on social, PR push | 50-60% |
| 9-10 | Urgency Build | Early-bird ends (if not already), countdown posts, email #5 (social proof), retargeting ads | 70-80% |
| 11-12 | Final Push | Last-chance emails, FOMO content, direct outreach, final social push | 90-100% |

### Global Audience Timezone Strategy

For multi-timezone virtual events (US/EU/APAC): choose one of three approaches — (1) single live session optimized for largest audience segment + promoted replay for others, (2) dual live sessions 8-12 hours apart, or (3) replay-first strategy with short live opening + prominent on-demand access. Adjust promotion messaging per timezone — lead with "live" for the target timezone and "on-demand within 24h" for others.

### Compressed Timeline (6 weeks)

| Week | Phase | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Launch | Website + registration live, invitation email, social announcement |
| 2-3 | Push | Speaker announcements, LinkedIn ads, partner sharing |
| 4 | Agenda | Full agenda release, email blast, session teasers |
| 5 | Urgency | "Almost full" messaging, social proof, direct outreach |
| 6 | Final | Last-chance email, countdown, personal outreach to key targets |

### Sprint Timeline (2-3 weeks, for meetups/small events)

| Week | Activities |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | Announce on all channels simultaneously (email + social + community). Open registration. |
| Week 2 | Reminder email, social posts, partner sharing, direct invites to key people |
| Week 3 | Final reminder, "last few spots" messaging, day-of logistics email |

## Channel Frequency by Phase

| Channel | Soft Launch | Active Promotion | Final Push |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email | 1 per week | 1-2 per week | 2-3 per week |
| LinkedIn (organic) | 2 per week | 3-4 per week | Daily |
| Twitter/X | 2-3 per week | Daily | 2-3 per day |
| LinkedIn (paid) | Start campaigns | Optimize, scale winners | Increase budget on top performers |
| Blog | 1 post | 1-2 per week | Recap/preview posts |
| Partner channels | Initial ask | Provide content to share | Final push reminder to partners |

**Diminishing returns warning:** More than 3 promotional emails per week causes unsubscribes. Switch from email to social and direct outreach in the final push.

## Registration Milestone Targets

Track weekly to detect underperformance early:

| Weeks Before Event | Cumulative Target | Action if Behind |
|---|---|---|
| 10-12 weeks | 10-15% | Normal — still early. Validate messaging. |
| 8-9 weeks | 25-35% | Review channel performance. Increase paid spend on top channel. |
| 6-7 weeks | 40-55% | Concern. Activate speaker networks. Add partner co-promotion. Consider pricing adjustment. |
| 4-5 weeks | 60-75% | Alert. Launch direct outreach to target accounts. Increase ad budget 50%. Email blitz. |
| 2-3 weeks | 80-90% | Critical. Personal invitations from leadership. "Last seats" messaging. Consider extending early-bird. |
| 1 week | 90-100% | Final push. On-site registration expected to add 5-10% at paid events. |

**If at 60% or below with 4 weeks to go:** Consider scope adjustment (smaller venue, fewer tracks) rather than panic marketing that damages brand.

## Budget Allocation by Channel

### For events with marketing budget

| Channel | % of Marketing Budget | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Email marketing (platform + design) | 10-15% | Highest ROI, owned audience |
| LinkedIn ads | 25-35% | B2B targeting, awareness + registration |
| Google Ads (search) | 10-15% | Capturing active search intent |
| Content creation (blog, video) | 10-15% | SEO, thought leadership |
| Retargeting ads | 10-15% | Converting website visitors |
| Design and creative | 10-15% | Visual assets across all channels |
| PR / media | 5-10% | Large conferences, product launches |

### For zero-budget events

Prioritize (in order):
1. Email to existing list (free)
2. Personal LinkedIn posts from team members (free)
3. Speaker network amplification (ask speakers to share)
4. Community channels (Slack, Discord, Meetup.com, Reddit)
5. Partner co-promotion (swap promotion with complementary events)
6. Referral incentive (discount or perk for bringing colleagues)
