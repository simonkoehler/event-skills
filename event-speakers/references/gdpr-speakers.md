## Lawful Basis for Speaker Data

| Processing Activity | Lawful Basis | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| CFP submission data (name, email, bio, abstract) | Legitimate interest | Necessary to evaluate proposals |
| Speaker contract and logistics (travel, accommodation) | Contract performance | Directly tied to speaker agreement |
| Publishing speaker bio and photo on website | Consent | Explicit consent required — include in speaker agreement |
| Recording sessions for on-demand replay | Consent | Separate consent per recording, not bundled with speaking agreement |
| Sharing speaker details with sponsors | Consent | Explicit, per-sponsor consent — never blanket sharing |
| Marketing use of speaker name and likeness | Consent or legitimate interest | Event promotion = legitimate interest; post-event marketing = consent |
| Speaker feedback and evaluation scores | Legitimate interest | Anonymize before sharing with third parties |

## CFP Data Collection

- Collect only what is needed to evaluate proposals: name, email, company, job title, bio, abstract, session preferences
- Do NOT require: home address, date of birth, social media handles (offer as optional)
- State data retention period in the CFP submission form
- Declined proposals: delete personal data within 6 months unless speaker consents to being contacted for future events
- Accepted proposals: retain data for the duration of the speaker agreement plus 12 months

**CFP privacy statement (include on submission form):**
"Your submission data (name, email, company, bio, abstract) will be reviewed by the program committee. If your proposal is not selected, your data will be deleted within 6 months. If selected, your data will be retained for the duration of the event and 12 months afterward for administrative purposes. You can request deletion at any time by contacting {{organizer_email}}."

## Speaker Privacy Notice

Include with every speaker invitation and in the speaker agreement:

1. Data controller name and contact
2. What data is collected (bio, photo, session content, travel details)
3. Purpose of each data use (event delivery, website, marketing, recording)
4. Who receives the data (event platform, AV partner, marketing team, sponsors if applicable)
5. Retention period per data type
6. Speaker rights (access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection)
7. Right to withdraw consent (specifically for recording and photo use)
8. Supervisory authority contact

## Recording and Photography Consent

Recording consent must be separate from the speaking agreement — a speaker can agree to present without agreeing to be recorded.

**Recording consent form fields:**
- [ ] I consent to my session being recorded for on-demand replay on the event platform (available for {{retention_period}})
- [ ] I consent to my session recording being used in post-event marketing materials (social clips, highlight reels)
- [ ] I consent to photographs taken during my session being used on the event website and social media

**Speaker rights regarding recordings:**
- Speaker may request recording removal at any time
- Speaker receives a copy of their recording within 14 days of the event
- Recording is not shared with sponsors unless speaker gives separate consent
- Recording is deleted after the stated retention period (default: 12 months)

## Speaker Bio Publishing

Publishing a speaker bio on the event website constitutes processing personal data. Requirements:

- Obtain explicit consent for the specific bio text, photo, and any linked profiles
- Speaker must approve the final published version (not just the raw submission)
- Provide a mechanism for speakers to update or remove their bio at any time
- After the event: archive speaker bios for 12 months, then delete unless speaker consents to continued listing
- Social media tagging: obtain consent before tagging speakers in posts that include personal details beyond name and session title

## International Speaker Transfers

When inviting speakers from outside the EU/EEA to speak at EU events (or vice versa):

- EU speakers at non-EU events: inform speakers how their data will be handled under non-EU jurisdiction
- Non-EU speakers at EU events: GDPR applies to their data while processed in connection with the EU event
- Cross-border data transfers: if using non-EU platforms for speaker management, ensure adequate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses or adequacy decision)
- Speaker agreements should specify governing data protection law

## Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention | Deletion Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| CFP submissions (declined) | 6 months post-notification | Automatic or on speaker request |
| CFP submissions (accepted) | Event date + 12 months | Automatic |
| Speaker agreement and logistics | Event date + 24 months | Legal retention requirement |
| Speaker bio on website | Event date + 12 months | Remove from public site, archive internally |
| Session recordings | As stated in consent (default 12 months) | Automatic or on speaker request |
| Speaker feedback/evaluation | Event date + 12 months | Anonymize after analysis |
| Speaker photos | Event date + 12 months | Delete or request re-consent |

## Speaker GDPR Checklist

**Pre-CFP:**
- [ ] Privacy statement included on CFP submission form
- [ ] Data retention period stated
- [ ] Lawful basis documented for proposal review

**Post-Selection:**
- [ ] Speaker privacy notice sent with invitation/agreement
- [ ] Recording consent form separate from speaking agreement
- [ ] Photo/bio publishing consent obtained
- [ ] Speaker approved final bio text and photo

**Pre-Event:**
- [ ] Speakers briefed on event photography and recording
- [ ] Opt-out mechanism available (no-photo badge or lanyard)
- [ ] AV partner has signed DPA if handling recordings

**Post-Event:**
- [ ] Recordings stored per consent terms
- [ ] Declined-speaker data flagged for deletion at 6-month mark
- [ ] Speaker bios archived per retention schedule
- [ ] Speakers notified of recording availability
