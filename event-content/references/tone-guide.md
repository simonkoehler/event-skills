## Audience Tone Presets

### Executive / C-Suite

**Characteristics:** Strategic, authoritative, concise, ROI-focused
**Sentence length:** Short. 15-20 words average.
**Rhythm:** Short sentences dominate. Declarative. Data first, conclusion second. Occasional two-word sentence for emphasis. "Revenue grew 40%. That matters."
**Vocabulary:** Business impact, competitive advantage, market leadership, strategic investment
**What works:** Data points, outcomes, peer references ("Join 200+ CTOs"), time-efficient messaging
**What fails:** Technical jargon, lengthy descriptions, vague benefits, overselling

**Example — email subject:** "3 strategies to accelerate pipeline — from 200 revenue leaders"
**Example — session description:** "Gain actionable strategies to drive 3x pipeline growth and align your go-to-market teams for 2026. Based on research across 500 B2B organizations."
**Example — social post:** "Every CTO we spoke to said the same thing: their #1 challenge isn't technology — it's alignment. At {{event_name}}, we're tackling it head-on."

---

### Developer / Technical

**Characteristics:** Direct, practical, no-fluff, peer-to-peer
**Sentence length:** Medium. Clear and specific.
**Rhythm:** Medium, precise. Technical terms need no explanation. Fragments work: "No config needed." or "Ships Monday." Mix longer explanatory sentences with terse conclusions.
**Vocabulary:** Technical terms welcome (Kubernetes, API, microservices), avoid marketing buzzwords
**What works:** Code examples, architecture diagrams, "show me, don't tell me," honest complexity
**What fails:** Buzzwords (synergy, leverage, empower), vague promises, stock photos, exclamation marks

**Example — email subject:** "Deep dive: Building scalable event-driven systems — live demos included"
**Example — session description:** "Deep-dive into building scalable microservices with Kubernetes — with live code demos and architecture patterns you can deploy Monday morning. Covers service mesh, observability, and failure handling."
**Example — social post:** "Our most requested session is back: hands-on Kubernetes workshop. Bring your laptop. Leave with a working deployment pipeline. #{{event_hashtag}}"

---

### Sales / Marketing

**Characteristics:** Outcome-focused, energetic, networking-emphasis
**Sentence length:** Medium-short. Punchy. Can use fragments.
**Rhythm:** Punchy. Fragment-heavy. Numbers standalone. "$2.4M pipeline. Two days. Your move." Longer setup sentence, then short payoff. Energy builds through brevity.
**Vocabulary:** Revenue, pipeline, conversion, ROI, growth, results, wins, community
**What works:** Social proof, success stories, FOMO, specific numbers ("$2.4M pipeline generated")
**What fails:** Abstract theory, overly technical content, passive voice, slow builds

**Example — email subject:** "The ABM playbook that generated $2.4M in pipeline — live at {{event_name}}"
**Example — session description:** "Learn the exact ABM playbook that generated $2.4M in pipeline at a mid-market SaaS company. Walk away with templates, targeting frameworks, and a 90-day implementation plan."
**Example — social post:** "300+ revenue leaders. 2 days of strategy. 1 goal: close more deals. {{event_name}} is where pipeline gets built. Secure your spot."

---

### Partner / Channel

**Characteristics:** Relationship-focused, collaborative, opportunity-driven
**Sentence length:** Medium. Warm but professional.
**Rhythm:** Flowing but warm. Longer relationship-building sentences, then a short, direct commitment ask. "Let's build this together." Mix collaborative framing with concrete asks.
**Vocabulary:** Partnership, co-selling, mutual growth, ecosystem, opportunity, collaboration
**What works:** Shared goals, success stories, exclusive access, relationship investment
**What fails:** One-sided value propositions, transactional language, hard-sell tactics

**Example — email subject:** "Grow your practice with us — exclusive partner sessions at {{event_name}}"
**Example — session description:** "Explore new co-selling opportunities and learn strategies to grow your practice. This partner-exclusive session covers joint go-to-market planning, deal registration optimization, and partner success stories."
**Example — social post:** "Our partners are the backbone of our ecosystem. At {{event_name}}, we're investing in your growth with exclusive workshops, 1:1 strategy sessions, and co-selling playbooks."

## Channel Tone Mapping

Same audience, different tone intensity by channel:

| Channel | Tone Register | Example Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| Website hero | Enthusiastic, aspirational | "Where leaders shape what's next" |
| Session descriptions | Informative, action-oriented | "You'll learn to identify, apply, and evaluate..." |
| Email — promotional | Persuasive, benefit-driven | "Don't miss the session that changed how we think about..." |
| Email — informational | Helpful, clear, practical | "Here's everything you need for event day" |
| Social — LinkedIn | Professional, thought-leadership | Paragraph form, insight-first |
| Social — Twitter/X | Concise, punchy, real-time | Fragment sentences, emojis OK |
| Social — Instagram | Visual-first, casual-professional | Short caption, emoji, hashtag-heavy |
| MC script | Warm, energetic, conversational | Spoken cadence, short sentences |
| Internal docs | Professional, precise, actionable | Bullet points, deadlines, responsibilities |
| Sponsor comms | Professional, opportunity-focused | ROI language, deliverables, data |

## Brand Consistency Framework

Maintain consistency across 20+ touchpoints with a four-level messaging hierarchy:

### Level 1: Core Event Message (max 25 words)
One sentence that appears in every communication. Captures the event's reason to exist.
Example: "{{event_name}} brings together {{audience_descriptor}} to [solve/explore/build] [core value proposition]."

### Level 2: Messaging Pillars (3-5)
Expand the core message into benefit categories. Each pillar gets its own supporting copy.
Example pillars:
- "World-class content from practitioners, not just pundits"
- "Curated networking designed to create real connections"
- "Hands-on workshops you can apply immediately"

### Level 3: Proof Points
Specific evidence supporting each pillar (stats, credentials, testimonials).
Example: "Last year, 92% of attendees rated sessions 'highly actionable'"

### Level 4: Audience Variants
Same message, adapted for each audience persona.

### Boilerplate Copy Library

Generate three lengths for standard use across materials:

**25-word boilerplate:**
"{{event_name}} is the premier {{industry}} event for {{audience_descriptor}}. {{event_date_display}} | {{venue_city}}. {{expected_attendees}}+ attendees expected."

**50-word boilerplate:**
Expand with value proposition and key content highlight.

**100-word boilerplate:**
Expand with speaker highlights, track overview, and registration CTA.

## Words and Phrases to Use and Avoid

### Use (by audience)

| All audiences | Executive | Developer | Sales |
|---|---|---|---|
| Actionable | Strategic | Hands-on | Pipeline |
| Practical | ROI-driven | Open-source | Revenue |
| Proven | Data-backed | Architecture | Conversion |
| Expert-led | Competitive advantage | Production-grade | Growth |
| Community | Board-level | Scalable | Wins |

### Avoid (all audiences)

| Word/Phrase | Why | Use Instead |
|---|---|---|
| Synergy | Overused, meaningless | Collaboration, partnership |
| Leverage | Jargon | Use, apply, build on |
| Thought leader | Self-aggrandizing | Expert, practitioner, leader |
| Disruptive | Overused | Innovative, transformative, new approach |
| Circle back | Corporate-speak | Follow up, revisit |
| Deep dive (overuse) | Fine once per document, not in every description | Detailed session, hands-on workshop |
| "Don't miss!" | Overused urgency | Be specific about what they'll miss |
| "In this day and age" | Filler | Cut entirely |
