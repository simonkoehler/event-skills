## Press Release Template

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
[or: EMBARGOED UNTIL {{embargo_date}}, {{embargo_time}} {{timezone}}]

{{headline — max 80 characters, no jargon, active voice}}

{{subheadline — adds context or a key data point}}

{{city}}, {{date}} — {{company_name}} today announced {{what — one sentence, 
plain language, newsworthy angle}}. {{Why it matters — one sentence connecting 
to market trend, customer impact, or industry shift}}.

[Paragraph 2: Key details. What specifically is being announced? Numbers, 
dates, availability. If product: what it does, who it's for, when available. 
If event: what happened, who participated, key outcome.]

[Paragraph 3: Quote from company executive. First person, attributed. 
"[Name], [Title] at [Company], said: '[Quote that adds perspective, 
not just repeats paragraph 1].'"]

[Paragraph 4: Supporting detail. Customer quote, partner endorsement, 
or additional context. If available: analyst quote or industry data point.]

[Paragraph 5: Call to action. Where to learn more, how to sign up, 
when it's available.]

### About {{company_name}}
[Boilerplate — 3-4 sentences. What the company does, key facts (founded, 
HQ, customers, funding/revenue if public). Update quarterly.]

### Media Contact
{{contact_name}}
{{contact_title}}
{{contact_email}}
{{contact_phone}}
```

**Rules:**
- Lead with the news, not the company history
- Headline: active voice, present tense, no exclamation marks
- First paragraph answers: What? Who? When? Why does anyone care?
- One executive quote maximum. Two if customer quote included.
- Under 500 words total. Journalists don't read longer.

## Press Kit Structure

Assemble digitally (shared folder or press page). Physical press kits only for major launches.

| Item | Format | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Press release | PDF + plain text | Both formats — journalists copy-paste from plain text |
| Fact sheet | 1-page PDF | Company facts, product facts, key numbers. Dense, no prose. |
| Executive bios | 100-word per exec | Only quoted or presenting executives |
| High-res images | JPEG, 300dpi, min 2000px | Product shots, executive headshots, event photos, logo |
| Logo pack | SVG + PNG (light/dark) | On white, on dark, icon-only variants |
| B-roll / video | MP4, download link | If product demo or event footage available |
| Background document | 2-3 page PDF | Deep context: market data, technical details, history — for journalists who want to go deeper |

**Distribution:** Email to press list with download link. Don't attach — files are too large.

## Q&A Document Template

Prepare BEFORE the press conference. Internal document — never distribute to media.

```
# Q&A Document: {{announcement_topic}}
# Internal Only — Not for Distribution

## Tier 1: Expected Questions (prepare answers for all)

Q: What exactly are you announcing today?
A: [Clear, quotable answer. 2-3 sentences max.]

Q: When is this available / when does this take effect?
A: [Specific date or timeline.]

Q: How much does it cost / what's the pricing?
A: [If public: state it. If not: "Pricing details will be shared with 
customers directly. We can say it's competitive with [benchmark]."]

Q: How is this different from [competitor]?
A: [Never name competitors. "Our approach differs in [specific way]. 
We focus on [differentiator]."]

Q: What problem does this solve?
A: [Customer-centric answer. Reference a real pain point.]

## Tier 2: Challenging Questions (prepare but hope you don't need)

Q: Why now? What took so long?
A: [Honest framing. "We prioritized [X] to ensure [quality/readiness]."]

Q: [Company-specific sensitive topic]
A: [Prepared answer. If truly sensitive: "We're not commenting on that 
today. Happy to follow up separately."]

Q: What if this fails / what are the risks?
A: [Acknowledge honestly, then pivot to mitigation.]

## Tier 3: Off-Limits / Bridge Statements

For topics you cannot or should not address:

- Financial projections: "We don't provide forward-looking financial guidance."
- Unannounced products: "We're focused on today's announcement. Stay tuned 
  for future updates."
- Personnel matters: "We don't comment on individual personnel decisions."
- Legal/regulatory: "That's a matter we're addressing through appropriate 
  channels."

## Bridge Technique
When a question goes off-topic: "That's an interesting point. What I can 
tell you about today's announcement is [pivot back to key message]."
```

## Embargo Communication

**Embargo = agreement that information is shared in advance but cannot be published before a specific date/time.**

### Embargo Email Template

```
Subject: EMBARGOED: {{company_name}} — {{announcement_topic}} — {{embargo_lift_date}}

Dear {{journalist_name}},

Under embargo until {{embargo_date}} at {{embargo_time}} {{timezone}}:

[Brief summary of announcement — 2-3 sentences]

Attached: Full press release, fact sheet, high-res images.

Embargo terms: This information may not be published, shared on social media, 
or disclosed in any form before {{embargo_date}} at {{embargo_time}} {{timezone}}.

Please confirm receipt and acceptance of embargo terms by replying to this email.

Available for interview: {{spokesperson_name}}, {{spokesperson_title}}
To schedule: reply to this email or call {{phone}}.

{{sender_name}}
{{sender_title}}
```

**Embargo rules:**
- Only offer embargoes to trusted journalists with track record of honoring them
- Always get written confirmation of embargo acceptance before sharing
- If embargo is broken by one outlet: lift for all immediately, notify all
- Standard embargo lead time: 24-72 hours before public announcement
- Embargo is a relationship tool, not a legal contract. It works on trust.

## Media Accreditation

For events with press attendance. Replaces standard registration.

**Accreditation form fields:**
- Journalist name
- Publication / outlet name
- Media type (print, online, broadcast, podcast, freelance)
- Published work samples (2-3 links to verify legitimacy)
- Photographer? (Y/N — affects access and equipment rules)
- Equipment being brought (cameras, tripods, lighting — venue may restrict)

**Accreditation process:**
1. Application via dedicated form (not the attendee registration page)
2. Verify legitimacy (check publication, review recent work)
3. Approve or decline within 5 business days
4. Send press badge + press kit access link
5. On-site: dedicated press check-in, press room with WiFi and power

**Press badge benefits:** Access to all sessions, press room, interview scheduling, press kit, photo opportunities. Usually complimentary (no registration fee).
