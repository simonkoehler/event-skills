# Sustainability Checklist

Load this reference when selecting venues, briefing vendors, or planning event operations. Sustainability is a top-3 industry trend as of 2025-2026 — attendees and sponsors increasingly expect it, and some corporate attendees require it for travel approval.

## Venue and Location

- [ ] Venue has environmental certification (ISO 14001, Green Globe, EMAS, or local equivalent)
- [ ] Venue accessible by public transport (reduces attendee car/taxi travel)
- [ ] Hotel partners within walking distance (no shuttle buses needed)
- [ ] Venue uses renewable energy or has carbon offset program
- [ ] Natural lighting available (reduces energy consumption and improves attendee experience)

## Catering

- [ ] Default menu is plant-forward (not exclusively, but meat is opt-in rather than default)
- [ ] Local and seasonal sourcing for food and beverages
- [ ] No single-use plastic: plates, cups, cutlery, bottles all reusable or compostable
- [ ] Water stations with reusable cups instead of individual plastic bottles
- [ ] Food waste reduction plan (smaller portions with refill option, donation of surplus)
- [ ] Allergen and dietary labels clearly displayed at every serving point

## Materials and Print

- [ ] Digital-first: event app replaces printed program (offer print only on request)
- [ ] Badges made from recyclable or compostable materials
- [ ] Lanyards collected for reuse at end of event
- [ ] Signage designed for reuse (no event-specific dates on permanent-style signs)
- [ ] Swag: only items with genuine utility. No plastic trinkets. Consider digital alternatives.
- [ ] Name badges: recycled card stock, no plastic holders unless reusable

## Travel and Carbon

- [ ] Carbon offset option on registration form (partner with certified offset provider)
- [ ] Virtual attendance option reduces travel need
- [ ] Priority to local and regional speakers (reduces flights)
- [ ] Group transportation from airport/station (shuttle instead of individual taxis)
- [ ] Bike parking at venue (for local attendees)

## Waste Management

- [ ] Clearly labeled recycling and waste stations throughout venue
- [ ] Composting for food waste (coordinate with caterer)
- [ ] Post-event cleanup plan with waste tracking (kg of waste, recycling rate)
- [ ] Exhibitor move-out: plan for material reuse, not landfill

## Reporting

Include in post-event report (see event-analytics):
- Estimated CO2 footprint (use online calculators: attendee travel + venue energy + catering)
- Waste metrics (total kg, recycling rate %)
- Sustainability initiatives implemented and their impact
- Recommendations for improvement at next edition

## Quick Wins (Low Effort, High Visibility)

These require almost no extra budget but signal commitment:
1. No single-use plastic bottles (water carafes cost less anyway)
2. Digital program instead of print (saves the per-person print cost entirely)
3. Plant-forward default menu (often cheaper than meat-heavy)
4. Badge lanyard collection box at exit (100% reuse rate achievable)
5. Public transport info prominent on event website (reduces taxi requests)
