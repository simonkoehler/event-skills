## Critical Path Dependencies

These dependencies are universal across event types. Tasks on the critical path cannot be parallelized — a delay in any one pushes all downstream tasks. Mark critical-path tasks with [CP] when generating timelines.

**Primary critical path (in-person events):**
Objectives defined → Date selected → Venue confirmed → Registration opens → Marketing launches → Speakers confirmed → Agenda published → Run-of-show finalized → Event executes → Post-event follow-up

**Primary critical path (virtual events):**
Objectives defined → Platform selected → Registration opens → Marketing launches → Speakers confirmed → Agenda published → Tech rehearsal completed → Event executes → Post-event follow-up

**Primary critical path (hybrid events):**
Objectives defined → Date selected → Venue confirmed + Platform selected → Registration opens → Marketing launches → Speakers confirmed → Agenda published → Dual run-of-show finalized → Full hybrid rehearsal → Event executes → Post-event follow-up

**Key dependency gates:**
1. **Venue confirmed** GATES: floor plan design, catering selection, AV vendor booking, exhibitor allocation, attendee capacity decisions
2. **Registration open** GATES: marketing launch, early-bird pricing, sponsor visibility
3. **Speakers confirmed** GATES: agenda publication, marketing speaker announcements, session logistics planning
4. **Agenda finalized** GATES: run-of-show creation, printed materials production, event app configuration
5. **Catering final numbers** GATE: must be submitted 48-72h before event (hard vendor deadline)
6. **Platform selected** (virtual/hybrid) GATES: speaker tech kits, attendee guide, virtual engagement setup, streaming configuration

When generating timelines, mark critical-path tasks with [CP] and note which downstream tasks they gate.

## Standard Conference Timeline

For conferences of 200-2,000 attendees with 12 months lead time.

### Phase 1: Concept (T-12 to T-9 months)

| Week | Task | Workstream | Dependencies |
|---|---|---|---|
| T-12 | Define event objectives and 3-5 measurable KPIs | Strategy | — |
| T-12 | Identify executive sponsor and secure sign-off | Strategy | — |
| T-11 | Set budget envelope (total and per major category) | Finance | Objectives defined |
| T-11 | Research and shortlist 3-5 potential dates | Logistics | — |
| T-11 | Research and shortlist 3-5 venues, request proposals | Logistics | Dates shortlisted |
| T-10 | Define target audience personas (2-3 profiles) | Strategy | Objectives defined |
| T-10 | Set registration target and pricing strategy | Registration | Budget set, audience defined |
| T-10 | Conduct venue site visits (top 2-3 candidates) | Logistics | Venue proposals received |
| T-9 | Finalize venue selection and sign contract | Logistics | Site visits completed |
| T-9 | Assemble full event team, assign workstream leads | Operations | — |
| T-9 | Create master project plan with milestones | Operations | Team assembled |
| T-9 | Draft initial sponsorship prospectus | Sponsors | Budget set, venue confirmed |

### Phase 2: Planning (T-9 to T-3 months)

| Week | Task | Workstream | Dependencies |
|---|---|---|---|
| T-9 | Open Call for Papers / begin speaker outreach | Content | Objectives defined |
| T-8 | Develop event brand identity (name, logo, visual language) | Marketing | — |
| T-8 | Build event website with initial content | Marketing | Brand identity |
| T-8 | Begin sponsor outreach (Platinum/Gold tiers first) | Sponsors | Prospectus drafted |
| T-7 | Launch early-bird registration | Registration | Website live, pricing set |
| T-7 | Set up registration platform and attendee data flows | Registration | — |
| T-7 | Begin marketing campaign (save-the-date, early promotion) | Marketing | Website live |
| T-6 | Close CFP, begin proposal review | Content | CFP period ended |
| T-6 | Book key vendors: AV/production, catering, photographer | Logistics | Venue confirmed |
| T-6 | Finalize sponsorship packages and exhibitor guide | Sponsors | — |
| T-5 | Select sessions and notify speakers (accept/reject) | Content | CFP review complete |
| T-5 | Draft preliminary agenda structure (tracks, time blocks) | Content | Sessions selected |
| T-5 | Close Platinum/Gold sponsor sales, begin Silver/Bronze | Sponsors | — |
| T-4 | Ramp up marketing (speaker announcements, content teasers) | Marketing | Speakers confirmed |
| T-4 | Order event materials (badges, signage, printed programs) | Logistics | Brand identity final |
| T-4 | Close early-bird registration, open standard pricing | Registration | — |
| T-3 | Confirm all speaker logistics (travel, accommodation, AV needs) | Content | Speakers confirmed |
| T-3 | First sponsor fulfillment check (logos received, booth specs confirmed) | Sponsors | — |
| T-3 | Review registration numbers against target, adjust marketing if needed | Registration | — |

### Phase 3: Execution Prep (T-3 to T-1 month)

| Week | Task | Workstream | Dependencies |
|---|---|---|---|
| T-3 | Finalize complete agenda with session times and rooms | Content | All speakers confirmed |
| T-3 | Create run-of-show document (minute-by-minute for event days) | Operations | Agenda finalized |
| T-2 | Send vendor briefs with final specifications | Logistics | Run-of-show drafted |
| T-2 | Collect all speaker presentations and review | Content | — |
| T-2 | Prepare attendee welcome pack (digital and/or physical) | Operations | — |
| T-2 | Brief all event staff on roles and responsibilities | Operations | Run-of-show final |
| T-2 | Test all technology (registration check-in, WiFi, streaming, AV) | Logistics | — |
| T-1 | Send final attendee communications (logistics email with venue directions, agenda, FAQ) | Marketing | Agenda final |
| T-1 | Conduct full rehearsal with speakers and AV team | Content | Presentations collected |
| T-1 | Final walkthrough with venue (room setups, signage placement, flow) | Logistics | — |
| T-1 | Print final materials (badges, programs, signage) | Logistics | Registration data frozen |
| T-1 | Confirm catering final numbers (usually 48-72h before event) | Logistics | Registration close date |
| T-1 week | Prepare emergency kit and contingency contact list | Operations | — |

### Phase 4: Event Day(s) (T-0)

| Time | Task | Workstream |
|---|---|---|
| Day before | Venue setup, signage installation, AV check | Logistics |
| Day before | Speaker check-in and green room setup | Content |
| Event morning | Registration desk opens (minimum 90 min before first session) | Registration |
| Event morning | Final AV and WiFi test in all session rooms | Logistics |
| Throughout | Session management (timekeeping, room monitoring, speaker support) | Content |
| Throughout | Social media coverage (live posts, photos, quotes) | Marketing |
| Throughout | Issue resolution (escalation path: staff → lead → event manager) | Operations |
| Throughout | Sponsor/exhibitor support | Sponsors |
| End of day | Secure equipment, back up recordings, brief team | Operations |

### Phase 5: Post-Event (T+1 day to T+4 weeks)

| Timeframe | Task | Workstream |
|---|---|---|
| T+1 day | Send thank-you emails to all attendees | Marketing |
| T+1 day | Send personalized thank-you to speakers and sponsors | Content / Sponsors |
| T+2 days | Distribute post-event survey | Analytics |
| T+1 week | Begin content repurposing (session recordings, slide decks, blog posts) | Content |
| T+1 week | Hand qualified leads to sales team with context | Registration |
| T+2 weeks | Collect and analyze survey results | Analytics |
| T+2 weeks | Conduct internal debrief meeting with full team | Operations |
| T+3 weeks | Complete financial reconciliation (actuals vs. budget) | Finance |
| T+4 weeks | Publish post-event ROI report | Analytics |
| T+4 weeks | Document lessons learned for next edition | Operations |

## Compressed Conference Timeline

For conferences with 6 months or less lead time. Merges Concept and Planning into a single phase with parallel workstreams.

### Phase 1: Concept + Planning (T-6 to T-3 months)

**Critical path items (sequential, cannot be parallelized):**
1. Define objectives and KPIs (Week 1)
2. Confirm venue and sign contract (Week 2-3)
3. Open registration (Week 4)

**Parallel workstreams (start immediately after objectives are defined):**
- Speaker outreach (skip formal CFP, invite directly)
- Sponsor outreach (simplified packages, faster close cycle)
- Marketing launch (website + initial campaign)
- Vendor booking (AV, catering, photography)

**Key adaptations for compressed timeline:**
- Skip formal CFP process — curate speakers through direct invitation
- Reduce sponsor tiers from 4 to 2-3
- Launch marketing and registration simultaneously
- Accept higher per-unit costs due to shorter vendor lead times
- Plan for 15-20% lower attendance vs. comparable event with full lead time

### Phases 3-5: Same as standard timeline

Execution Prep, Event Day, and Post-Event phases remain at full duration. Never compress Post-Event — the follow-up window is time-sensitive and drives ROI.

## Trade Show Timeline

For trade shows and expos (500-5,000+ attendees), 12-18 month lead time.

**Additional workstreams vs. standard conference:**
- Exhibitor management (booth allocation, exhibitor kit, move-in/move-out schedule)
- Floor plan design and optimization
- Lead capture system setup and testing
- Show guide / expo directory production
- Dedicated exhibitor communications sequence

**Key timeline additions:**

| When | Task |
|---|---|
| T-15 months | Develop floor plan with booth layout options |
| T-12 months | Open exhibitor sales with prospectus |
| T-9 months | First exhibitor allocation round |
| T-6 months | Publish exhibitor kit with technical specifications |
| T-4 months | Exhibitor deadline: logos, descriptions, electrical requirements |
| T-2 months | Produce show guide / expo directory |
| T-1 week | Publish move-in schedule with loading dock assignments |
| T-2 days | Exhibitor move-in begins |
| T+1 day | Exhibitor move-out (coordinate with venue) |

## Product Launch Timeline

For product launches (50-500 attendees), 3-6 month lead time.

**Differentiators:**
- Tight messaging control (all content approved by product/marketing leadership)
- Demo logistics (tech setup, demo stations, backup devices)
- Media coordination (press list, embargo management, press kit)
- Executive rehearsals (CEO/founder presentation runs need extra time)
- NDA management if pre-announcement

**Key timeline:**

| When | Task |
|---|---|
| T-6 months | Lock launch date with product team (coordinate with product readiness) |
| T-5 months | Develop messaging framework and key narratives |
| T-4 months | Book venue, begin media list development |
| T-3 months | Finalize invite list (customers, analysts, press, partners) |
| T-2 months | Send embargoed briefings to key analysts/press |
| T-6 weeks | Finalize all demo scenarios and backup plans |
| T-4 weeks | Executive presentation rehearsals (minimum 3 rounds) |
| T-2 weeks | Send press kits under embargo |
| T-1 week | Full technical rehearsal with all demos |
| T+0 | Embargo lifts, social media blitz, press follow-up |
| T+1 day | Analyst/press follow-up meetings |

## Sales Kickoff Timeline

For internal SKOs (100-2,000 attendees), 4-8 month lead time.

**Differentiators:**
- Internal audience (no external marketing needed, but internal comms critical)
- Training sessions (content developed with enablement team)
- Awards ceremony (nominations, judging, trophy/gift ordering)
- Motivation arc (agenda designed to energize, not just inform)
- Often tied to fiscal year start

**Key additions:**

| When | Task |
|---|---|
| T-8 months | Align with leadership on themes and messaging priorities |
| T-6 months | Book venue (often resort/hotel for multi-day) |
| T-4 months | Design training curriculum with enablement team |
| T-3 months | Open award nominations |
| T-2 months | Finalize breakout session content |
| T-1 month | Order awards, gifts, swag |
| T-2 weeks | Rehearse main stage presentations |

## Community Meetup Timeline

For meetups and small community events (20-200 attendees), 1-3 month lead time.

| When | Task |
|---|---|
| T-3 months | Define topic and format (talks, panel, workshop, unconference) |
| T-3 months | Secure venue (co-working space, company office, community center) |
| T-2 months | Confirm 2-3 speakers or facilitators |
| T-2 months | Set up registration page (Meetup.com, Eventbrite, Lu.ma) |
| T-6 weeks | Begin promotion (community channels, social media, newsletters) |
| T-4 weeks | Confirm catering/drinks (pizza + drinks is the standard) |
| T-2 weeks | Send reminder to registered attendees |
| T-1 week | Confirm final headcount with venue and catering |
| T-1 day | Test AV setup (projector, microphone if needed) |
| T+1 day | Post recap (photos, slides, recording if applicable) |
| T+1 week | Share with community, announce next meetup date |

## Roadshow Timeline

For multi-city roadshows (50-200 per stop), 3-6 months lead time.

**Generate a master plan plus per-city instance:**
- Master plan covers brand consistency, speaker prep, content creation
- Per-city plan covers local venue, local speakers (if any), local promotion, logistics

| When | Task |
|---|---|
| T-6 months | Define city list and sequence (typically 4-8 cities) |
| T-5 months | Book venues in all cities (negotiate multi-venue deal if possible) |
| T-4 months | Develop master content package (presentations, demos, collateral) |
| T-3 months | Localize content for each city if needed (language, case studies) |
| T-3 months | Open registration per city |
| T-2 months | City-specific promotion campaigns |
| T-1 month | Ship materials and equipment to first city |
| During | Post-city debrief after each stop, adjust for next city |
| T+1 week after last stop | Consolidated follow-up across all cities |

## Virtual Event Timeline

For virtual events (50-2,000 attendees), 4-8 weeks lead time. Timeline is 30-40% shorter than in-person equivalent.

**Key differences vs. in-person:**
- No venue tasks — replaced by platform selection, configuration, and testing
- No physical logistics — replaced by speaker tech kit shipping, virtual engagement setup
- Marketing timeline compressed but emphasis shifts to digital channels
- Tech rehearsals replace venue walkthroughs

| When | Task |
|---|---|
| T-8 weeks | Define objectives, format (webinar / multi-session / multi-day), and KPIs [CP] |
| T-7 weeks | Select and contract virtual event platform [CP] |
| T-6 weeks | Confirm speakers, begin content development |
| T-6 weeks | Build event landing page and open registration [CP] |
| T-5 weeks | Launch marketing campaign (email, social, paid) |
| T-4 weeks | Configure platform (sessions, breakout rooms, engagement features) |
| T-3 weeks | Ship speaker tech kits (camera, mic, ring light) if needed |
| T-3 weeks | Collect speaker presentations and review |
| T-2 weeks | Individual speaker tech checks (test audio, video, screen share) |
| T-1 week | Full technical rehearsal with all speakers and production team [CP] |
| T-3 days | Send attendee prep email (platform access, troubleshooting guide, agenda) |
| T-1 day | Final platform test, backup stream configured |
| T+0 | Event execution (dedicated tech support monitoring all sessions) |
| T+1 day | Thank-you emails, survey distribution, recording processing |

## Micro Event Timeline

For micro events (10-50 attendees, VIP dinners, executive roundtables), 2-6 weeks lead time.

| When | Task |
|---|---|
| T-4 weeks | Define purpose, curate guest list (quality over quantity) |
| T-3 weeks | Select venue/restaurant, negotiate private dining or meeting space |
| T-3 weeks | Send personalized invitations (email or physical) |
| T-2 weeks | Follow up on RSVPs, manage waitlist if needed |
| T-2 weeks | Plan conversation agenda or facilitation structure |
| T-1 week | Confirm final headcount with venue |
| T-1 week | Prepare place cards, any printed materials, gifts |
| T-3 days | Collect dietary requirements and communicate to venue |
| T-1 day | Final walkthrough, confirm setup and AV (if any) |
| T+1 day | Send personalized thank-you notes (handwritten or high-quality email) |
| T+1 week | Follow up on any commitments made during the event |

## Calibration Rules

When the user's available lead time does not match the standard template:

1. **50-75% of standard lead time:** Compress Phase 1 and 2 into one phase. Run workstreams in parallel. Flag increased risk.
2. **25-50% of standard lead time:** Sprint mode. Daily milestones. Reduce scope (fewer tracks, simpler format, smaller venue). Skip formal processes (CFP → direct invite, RFP → direct booking).
3. **<25% of standard lead time:** Emergency mode. Focus on minimum viable event. Explicitly list what to cut vs. keep. Flag that quality and attendance will be impacted.
4. **More lead time than standard:** Do not pad the timeline. Use extra time for higher-quality content curation, stronger marketing, and contingency buffer.
5. **DACH/premium venues:** Congress centers in Germany (CCH Hamburg, Messe München, ICM), Austria (Messe Wien), and Switzerland (KKL Luzern, Congress Center Basel) book 18-24 months in advance. Add 3-6 months to standard timelines for DACH events at premium venues.
