# Internal Event Workflows

Load this reference when the event type is a Sales Kickoff (SKO), Town Hall, All-Hands, internal training, or any event where the audience is employees rather than external attendees.

Internal events follow different rules than external events. No public registration. No demand gen. The audience is mandatory or strongly encouraged. The goal is alignment, motivation, or skill-building — not lead generation.

## Sales Kickoff

**Format:** 100-2,000 people, 1-3 days, typically Q1 (January-February). Mix of plenary, breakouts, training, and social.

**Lead time note for resort-style destinations:** For SKOs in popular destinations (Lisbon, Barcelona, Mallorca, Cyprus, Malta in winter; Iceland, Scotland in summer), venue booking lead time is 10-14 months, not the 4-8 months in the generic event-type matrix. The best 200+ pax resort venues fill 12 months ahead for January.

### What Makes SKOs Different

- **Audience is internal.** No registration funnel, no marketing. Attendance is expected, not sold.
- **Content is confidential.** Roadmap previews, deal reviews, competitive intel — cannot be shared externally.
- **Motivation is a core objective.** Not just information transfer — energy, alignment, culture.
- **Awards ceremonies** are a tradition. Plan 45-60 min. Need: categories, nominations process, trophies/awards, presentation scripts, walk-up music, photos.
- **Training sessions** are hands-on. Role plays, deal clinics, product demos, certification exercises.
- **Social programming** matters more than at conferences. Team dinners, activities, entertainment — these build the relationships that make remote teams work.

### SKO Agenda Building Blocks

| Block | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| CEO/Leadership keynote | 30-45 min | Vision, strategy, "where we're going" |
| Revenue review | 20-30 min | Last year's performance, this year's targets |
| Product roadmap | 30-45 min | What's coming, how to sell it |
| Competitive update | 20-30 min | Win/loss analysis, battlecards |
| Deal clinics | 60-90 min (breakout) | Small groups review real deals, practice objection handling |
| Skills training | 60-90 min (breakout) | New methodology, new tools, product demo skills |
| Customer panel | 30-45 min | Real customers on stage — why they bought, what matters |
| Awards ceremony | 45-60 min | Annual awards, recognition, celebration |
| Team-building activity | 2-4 hours | Offsite activity, competition, social bonding |
| 1:1 Manager-Rep (Comp Plan Sync) | 30-45 min per rep | Confidential quota/territory delivery, comp plan acknowledgment. Schedule one slot per rep across days 2-3 — do not skip. |

### SKO-Specific Checklist Additions

- [ ] Award categories defined and nominations collected (T-6 weeks)
- [ ] Trophies / awards ordered (T-4 weeks)
- [ ] Confidentiality reminder sent to all attendees (internal content, no social sharing)
- [ ] Team dinner seating plan (mix teams, don't let everyone sit with their own group)
- [ ] Entertainment / activity booked (T-8 weeks for popular options)
- [ ] Swag / kit bags prepared (branded, practical — not another generic t-shirt)

### SKO Pre-Work and Post-Work (Enablement + Compliance)

SKOs aren't just a 3-day event — they're book-ended by enablement and acknowledgment workstreams. Skip these and the comp-plan rollout has no audit trail.

**Pre-event (T-3 to T-1 weeks):**
- [ ] 60-90 min of pre-recorded enablement per rep: product positioning, competitive battlecards, new comp plan walkthrough video
- [ ] Required-completion tracked in LMS (target ≥95% completion before arrival)
- [ ] Comp plan document distributed (read-only) for review questions to be brought to the 1:1
- [ ] Pre-work survey: 5 questions on what reps want from the SKO (input feeds session design)

**Post-event (T+1 to T+2 weeks):**
- [ ] Knowledge check launched in LMS (15-25 questions, 80% pass required, two attempts)
- [ ] Comp plan acknowledgment signed in HRIS / DocuSign (legal requirement in most jurisdictions before commission accrual)
- [ ] Manager 1:1 to set Q1 plan (account list, pipeline target, key activities)
- [ ] Recording library available in LMS for absent reps (mandatory makeup-watch with manager attestation)
- [ ] Session content NOT shared externally (no LinkedIn, no public blog) — internal-only repurposing only

**Why this matters:** The new comp plan is a binding contract change. Without explicit acknowledgment in HRIS, reps can later claim they never agreed to the new quota or split — disputing commissions and creating legal risk. Treat SKO recordings as HR/compliance artifacts, not marketing content (different storage, different retention — typically 7 years for labor law jurisdictions).

## Town Hall / All-Hands

**Format:** 50-10,000 people, 60-90 minutes, usually hybrid (HQ live + remote stream). Monthly or quarterly.

### Town Hall Structure

| Time | Block | Lead |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:05 | Welcome, agenda overview | MC or Chief of Staff |
| 0:05-0:20 | CEO update (strategy, results, priorities) | CEO |
| 0:20-0:30 | Functional spotlight (rotating: product, sales, engineering, HR) | Functional VP |
| 0:30-0:40 | Employee spotlight / culture moment | HR or MC |
| 0:40-0:55 | Live Q&A | CEO + leadership team |
| 0:55-1:00 | Closing, next steps, next Town Hall date | MC |

### Hybrid Town Hall Specifics

- **Primary format:** Streaming for most employees, small live audience at HQ
- **Production:** Treat like a broadcast, not a meeting room call. Professional camera, good audio, producer managing transitions.
- **Q&A:** Collect questions digitally (Slido, Teams Q&A) — don't rely on "unmute and ask." Pre-seed 2-3 questions to avoid dead air at the start.
- **Recording:** Always record. Employees in different timezones watch on-demand. Make available within 2 hours.
- **Accessibility:** Live captions mandatory. Multiple language streams if global.
- **Engagement check:** Track live viewership and Q&A participation. If less than 60% of company watches (live or on-demand within 48h), the format needs rethinking.

### Town Hall Communication Plan

- T-2 weeks: Calendar invite to all employees (from CEO or CEO's EA)
- T-1 week: "Submit your questions" email with link to Q&A tool
- T-1 day: Reminder with agenda preview ("Here's what we'll cover")
- T+2 hours: Recording available + summary email with key points
- T+1 week: Follow-up on any commitments made during Q&A

## Internal Training Event

**Format:** 10-50 people, half-day to 2 days, focused on skill-building.

### Training-Specific Additions

- **Pre-work:** Distribute materials 1 week before. Assignments, reading, software to install.
- **Certification:** If the training leads to a certificate, define pass criteria, assessment method, and certificate template.
- **Hands-on ratio:** Minimum 50% of time is practice, not presentation. Use the 60/40 rule: 40% input, 60% activity.
- **Feedback:** Per-session rating (1-5) plus open text. Track knowledge gain with pre/post assessment (same 5 questions before and after).
- **Materials:** Printed workbooks or shared digital workspace. Not just slides — participants need something to write on, refer back to, and keep.

## Common Elements

### Internal Communication (replaces Marketing)

| External Event | Internal Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Email campaign to list | Email from CEO/CHRO to all employees |
| LinkedIn promotion | Slack/Teams announcement in #general |
| Paid advertising | Manager cascade ("ensure your team attends") |
| Registration page | Calendar invite (mandatory) |
| Early-bird pricing | N/A |
| Sponsor promotion | N/A |

### Attendance Management (replaces Registration)

- No registration system. Calendar invite is the "registration."
- Track RSVPs through calendar responses or a simple internal form.
- For hybrid: ask "Will you attend in-person or remotely?" — this drives catering and room setup.
- Badge printing: from HR employee list, not registration export.

### Budget Considerations

Internal events are often funded by HR, L&D, or the department — not marketing.

Use Town Hall as the 1.0× baseline per-person cost and scale by event type:

| Item | SKO (ratio to Town Hall) | Town Hall (1.0× baseline) | Training (ratio to Town Hall) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Venue | 2.5-10× | 1.0× (HQ or streaming) | 1.5-5× |
| Catering | 3-7×/day | 1.0× (coffee only) | 2-4× |
| Travel | High (flights + hotels) | None (mostly remote) | Moderate (regional travel) |
| Entertainment/activity | Significant | None | None |
| Awards/swag | Moderate | None | Minimal (certificates) |
| AV/production | 1.5-2× | 1.0× (streaming) | 0.5-0.6× |
| **Total** | **~16-18× Town Hall** | **1.0× baseline** | **~7× Town Hall** |
