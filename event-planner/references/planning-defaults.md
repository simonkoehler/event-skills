## Industry Cost Context

Flag these realities when generating budgets — they reflect the current market environment:

- Event costs have risen 40-50% since pre-pandemic levels (Forrester 2025)
- Two-thirds of B2B event budgets are flat or declining year-over-year (Marketing Week 2025)
- 59% of organizations are shifting to smaller, more targeted events (Forrester 2025)
- 50% of planners plan to use AI for ideation and agenda-building (Amex GBT 2026)
- 38% cite increasing costs as their top challenge (Amex GBT 2026)
- Exhibition industry revenues remain 15.6% below 2019 levels despite attendee recovery (CEIR 2025)
- Virtual events cost 50-75% less than equivalent in-person events
- Hybrid events typically cost 30-40% MORE than in-person alone due to dual production

When budget is tight, recommend in this priority order:
1. Reduce event scale (fewer attendees, shorter duration) before cutting quality
2. Simplify production (fewer tracks, simpler staging)
3. Shift to sponsor-funded model where possible
4. Consider virtual format to eliminate venue and travel costs
5. Never cut contingency below 10%

## Budget Category Breakdown

Default allocation for in-person events:

| Category | % of Total | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Venue (rental, rooms, basic AV) | 25-35% | Largest single line item |
| Food & beverage | 20-30% | Second largest; increases with event duration |
| AV / production / staging | 10-20% | Can spike for product launches and large stages |
| Marketing & promotion | 5-10% | Higher for first editions, lower for established events |
| Speaker costs (travel, fees, accommodation) | 5-15% | Paid keynotes drive this up significantly |
| Printed materials & signage | 3-5% | Declining as events go more digital |
| Staff & temporary labor | 5-10% | Registration, security, setup crew |
| Technology (event app, WiFi, streaming) | 3-8% | Higher for hybrid events |
| Contingency | 10-15% | Non-negotiable; covers overruns and emergencies |

## Budget Allocation by Format

Use this table to adjust default allocations based on event format:

| Category | In-Person | Virtual | Hybrid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Venue & F&B | 40-45% | 0-5% | 25-30% |
| Technology & production | 10-15% | 35-45% | 25-35% |
| Marketing & promotion | 15-20% | 20-25% | 15-20% |
| Speakers (fees, travel, kits) | 10-15% | 10-15% | 10-15% |
| Staff & travel | 10-15% | 5-10% | 10-15% |
| Contingency | 10-15% | 10-15% | 15-20% |

**Format-specific notes:**
- Virtual events eliminate venue costs but increase technology spend (platform licensing, streaming infrastructure, virtual engagement tools)
- Hybrid events are the most expensive format per attendee because they require full in-person production PLUS full virtual production
- For hybrid, budget an additional 15-20% contingency because dual-format introduces more failure points
- Virtual speakers may still need equipment kits shipped to them (camera, ring light, microphone — budget 0.5-1.5% of total event budget per remote speaker, or get quotes from local suppliers)

## Team Sizing Guidelines

| Event Size | Core Team | Extended Team | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| <50 attendees | 1 person | 1-2 volunteers | Event lead handles everything |
| 50-200 attendees | 1-2 people | 3-5 helpers on event day | Dedicated event lead + support |
| 200-500 attendees | 3-5 people | 8-15 on event day | Workstream leads for content, logistics, marketing |
| 500-1,000 attendees | 5-8 people | 15-30 on event day | Full team with clear RACI |
| 1,000-2,000 attendees | 8-12 people | 30-60 on event day | Project manager + workstream leads + coordinators |
| 2,000+ attendees | 12-20 people | 60-100+ on event day | Professional event management team or agency |

For hybrid events, add 2-3 people to the core team for virtual production (technical director, virtual engagement moderator, streaming engineer).

## Venue Capacity Rules of Thumb

| Setup | Per-Person Space Needed | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Theater style (rows of chairs) | 0.8-1.0 m² per person | Most efficient for keynotes |
| Classroom style (tables + chairs) | 1.5-2.0 m² per person | Needed for workshops, training |
| Banquet rounds (dining) | 1.5-1.8 m² per person | Standard for seated meals |
| Cocktail / standing reception | 0.6-0.8 m² per person | Most space-efficient for networking |
| Exhibition / trade show | 3.0-5.0 m² per person | Includes booth space and aisles |
| Boardroom / U-shape | 2.5-3.5 m² per person | Small group, premium feel |

## Registration Conversion Benchmarks

| Metric | Typical Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Website visit → registration | 5-15% | Higher for niche, invited events |
| Email open rate (general B2B) | 20-35% | Newsletters, drip campaigns, cold outreach |
| Email open rate (event-specific) | 36-42% | Event invitations, reminders, confirmations — higher due to targeted audience and time-sensitivity. Inflated by Apple Mail Privacy Protection — use CTR as more reliable signal |
| Email click-through rate | 2-5% | To registration page |
| Early-bird uptake | 20-35% of total registrations | Depends on discount depth |
| No-show rate (free events) | 30-50% | Always overbook free events |
| No-show rate (paid events) | 5-15% | Paid commitment reduces no-shows |
| No-show rate (virtual events) | 40-60% | Higher than in-person; overbook by 2x |
| Drop-off rate (multi-day) | 10-20% per additional day | Day 1 always has highest attendance |
| Virtual engagement drop-off | 20-30% after first hour | Plan for declining virtual audience |

## Post-Event Response Windows

| Action | Optimal Window | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Thank-you email | Within 24 hours | While experience is fresh |
| Survey distribution | Within 48 hours | Response rates drop 50% after 72h |
| Lead handoff to sales | Within 48 hours | Warm leads cool fast |
| Social media recap | Within 24-48 hours | Maximize engagement while event is trending |
| Content publishing (recordings) | Within 1-2 weeks | Extends event value and reach |
| Sponsor fulfillment report | Within 2 weeks | Demonstrates ROI, supports renewal conversation |
| Internal debrief | Within 2 weeks | Before organizational memory fades |
| ROI report | Within 4 weeks | Close the loop on objectives |
| Lessons learned document | Within 4 weeks | Capture institutional knowledge |
