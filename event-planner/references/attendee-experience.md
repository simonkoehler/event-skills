## Attendee Journey Map

Map every touchpoint from the attendee's perspective — not the planner's. Each phase has an emotional state, practical needs, and friction points to eliminate.

| Phase | Attendee Thinks | Deliver | Common Friction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-registration | "Is this worth my time and budget?" | Clear value proposition, agenda preview, social proof | Vague website, no agenda, no speaker names yet |
| Registration | "How do I sign up?" | Simple form, fast checkout, immediate confirmation | Too many fields, unclear pricing, slow page load |
| Pre-event (T-30 to T-1) | "What should I prepare?" | Logistics email, agenda, networking app invite, speaker info | Radio silence between registration and event |
| Arrival | "Where do I go?" | Clear signage, fast check-in, welcome, badge | Long queue, confusing venue layout, no greeter |
| First 30 minutes | "Do I belong here?" | Icebreaker, coffee, visible staff, event app onboarding | Standing alone with no one to talk to |
| During sessions | "Am I learning something useful?" | Quality content, audience interaction, comfortable room | Bad AV, no Q&A, too cold/hot, poor sight lines |
| Breaks | "Who should I meet?" | Facilitated networking, sponsor booths, good coffee | Awkward milling around, empty hallways |
| Meals | "Can I eat something I actually want?" | Dietary options, comfortable seating, conversation starters | Long queues, no dietary labels, nowhere to sit |
| End of day | "Was this worth it?" | Clear next steps, energy, community feeling | Whimper ending, no social event, unclear logistics |
| Post-event | "What did I get out of this?" | Thank-you, recordings, slides, follow-up, survey | No follow-up, broken recording links, spam |

## Networking Facilitation Methods

Don't just schedule "networking breaks" — facilitate actual connections.

### Speed Networking (works for 50-200 people)
- 3-minute paired conversations, bell rings, rotate
- Provide conversation prompts on table cards
- Works best in first 90 minutes of day 1 (breaks the ice for everything else)
- Needs: open space, timer, bell/gong, table cards with prompts

### Roundtable Rotation (works for 30-150 people)
- 8-person tables, each with a topic and facilitator
- 15-20 minutes per round, 3 rounds
- Attendees choose table by interest, not assignment
- Needs: round tables, topic signs, facilitator per table, timer

### Birds-of-a-Feather Tables (works at any meal)
- Lunch tables labeled by interest/topic
- Self-selecting — attendees sit where their interest is
- Low-effort, high-value. Just needs table signs.

### App-Based Matchmaking (works for 200+ people)
- Event app matches attendees by role, interest, or goals
- Attendees schedule 1:1 meetings in dedicated meeting areas
- Needs: event app with matching feature, meeting space with tables

### Facilitated Introductions (works for VIP/executive events)
- Event team pre-matches attendees based on profiles
- Personal introductions by host: "Sarah, you need to meet Tom — he just solved the exact problem you described"
- Highest effort, highest value. Only feasible for events under 100.

## Engagement Touchpoints

Beyond sessions and breaks — moments that shape how the event feels.

**Before arrival:**
- Personalized email from a real person (not a system), 48h before: "Looking forward to seeing you. Here's one thing to check out."
- Event app push notification: "Your personalized agenda is ready"

**At arrival:**
- Greeter at entrance (not just signage — a human who says hello)
- Welcome drink or snack at registration (reduces queue frustration)
- First-timer ribbon or badge marker (signals "please talk to me")

**During event:**
- Live polls in sessions (real-time audience participation, not just passive listening)
- Photo opportunity with event branding (attendees share on social media = free promotion)
- Charging station in high-traffic area (practical, and people linger and talk)
- Quiet room for introverts and those who need a break

**At closing:**
- Closing keynote or panel that ends on high energy (never end with logistics announcements)
- Swag or takeaway with genuine utility (not another tote bag)
- Clear "what happens next" (recordings, follow-up, next edition date)
