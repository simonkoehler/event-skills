# Executive & VIP Event Workflows

Load this reference when the event type is a micro event, VIP dinner, executive roundtable, advisory board meeting, or any event under 50 attendees with a curated guest list.

These events operate on different rules than conferences. No public registration. No mass marketing. Every touchpoint is personal.

## Executive Dinner

**Format:** 10-30 guests, seated dinner, structured conversation, 2.5-3 hours total.

### Planning Timeline (4-6 weeks)

| Timing | Action |
|---|---|
| T-6 weeks | Define guest profile, conversation topic, venue shortlist |
| T-5 weeks | Personal invitation (email from a senior leader, not marketing) |
| T-4 weeks | Follow-up calls to non-responders. Finalize venue. |
| T-3 weeks | Confirm guest list. Seating plan draft 1. Brief moderator. |
| T-2 weeks | Logistics email to confirmed guests (venue, dress code, dietary) |
| T-1 week | Final seating plan. Print place cards. Brief catering on dietary. |
| T-1 day | Venue walkthrough. Confirm AV if presenting. |
| Event day | Host arrives 60 min early. Greet each guest personally at entrance. |
| T+1 day | Personal thank-you email from host (not automated, actually personal). |
| T+1 week | Follow-up with any commitments made during conversation. |

### Invitation Approach

These are NOT mass emails. Each invitation is personal.

**Email from:** Named senior executive (CTO, VP, GM — not "Events Team")
**Tone:** Collegial. "I'm hosting a small dinner with 15 technology leaders to discuss [topic]. I'd value your perspective."
**No registration link.** Reply to RSVP. Maximum friction: reply to this email.
**Follow-up:** Phone call, not reminder email. "Hi Sarah, wanted to make sure you saw my dinner invitation."

### Acceptance Rate Expectations (the math that catches most planners off-guard)

**The single most common failure mode of executive roundtables is over-promising attendance.** A planner invites 30 CxOs hoping for 30 RSVPs and lands 8. The fix is structural: invite 1.5-3× your target depending on relationship temperature.

| Relationship type | Lead time | Expected acceptance rate | To land 30 confirmed, invite |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cold (never met, no prior touch) | 6+ weeks | 20-35% | 90-150 |
| Warm (LinkedIn connection, 1 prior touch) | 6+ weeks | 35-50% | 60-85 |
| Strong (existing customer or peer of host) | 4+ weeks | 50-70% | 45-60 |
| Personal ask by named exec | 3+ weeks | 60-80% | 38-50 |

**At <6 weeks lead time for CxO audiences:** acceptance rates drop further — CxO calendars typically lock 8-12 weeks out. Below that window, even strong relationships have 30-40% conflict-driven decline rates.

**Build a 1.5-3× invitation pipeline.** Track invitations, declines, and confirmations separately. Don't count "no reply" as "maybe" — count it as "no" after the first follow-up phone call.

**Lead-time risk flag (audience-aware):** Apply this in addition to the event-type lead-time check:
| Audience seniority | Minimum invitation lead time |
|---|---|
| C-suite (CxO, EVP) | 8-12 weeks |
| VP / Senior Director | 6-8 weeks |
| Director and below | 4-6 weeks |

If the user's lead time is below the audience-tier minimum, flag the risk explicitly, even if the event-type's typical lead time would allow shorter.

### Seating Plan

This is where executive dinners succeed or fail.

**Rules:**
- No couple seating (people who already know each other sit apart)
- Mix industries and roles for diverse conversation
- Host sits center, not head of table — facilitates, doesn't dominate
- Place cards with first name and company (no titles — keeps it peer-level)
- Pre-brief the host on each guest: one conversation starter per person

### Conversation Design

- **No slide decks.** Executives don't want another presentation over dinner.
- **Moderator-led discussion** with 3-5 prepared questions
- **Chatham House Rules:** "You can share what was discussed but not who said it."
- **Document:** One person takes notes (discreetly, not on laptop). Summarize into 1-page insight brief post-event.

### Vocabulary discipline (closed-door / NDA / Chatham House events)

For executive dinners, advisory boards, closed-door roundtables, and any format where the brief specifies "no commercial vocabulary" or "off-record / Chatham House Rules": scan the **entire output** before submitting for forbidden vocabulary.

**Forbidden terms (binary test — even one occurrence = fail):**

- sales, pipeline, opportunity, MQL, SQL, lead, deal, ARR, ACV
- conversion, qualification, next steps, prospect, funnel
- pipeline-build, lead-gen, close, win, nurture, score, attribution
- ROI, KPI, revenue, quota, demo, pitch, sell, sold, buying signal
- CRM update, sales handover, account-based marketing (ABM)

These terms must not appear **anywhere** in the output — not in operational instructions, not in negation framing ("we will not pursue pipeline"), not in unlock-matrix questions, not in checklist items. If the rule is binary, the scan is binary.

**Neutral-language replacements:**

| Forbidden | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "pipeline of names" / "pipeline size" | "list of names" / "list size" |
| "build a pipeline of N invitees" | "build an invitation list of N names" |
| "nurture sequence" | "follow-up cadence" / "relationship-care sequence" |
| "qualify the leads" | "identify the right voices for the room" |
| "conversion rate" | "acceptance rate" / "RSVP rate" |
| "next steps after the meeting" | "follow-through commitments" |
| "ROI of the dinner" | "value of the conversation" |
| "MQL / SQL" | "warm contact / strong relationship" |
| "demo of the product" | "discussion of the capability" |
| "sales handover" | "introductory connection" |

**Where this rule applies and where it doesn't:**

- ✅ Applies to executive dinners, advisory boards, closed-door roundtables, off-record customer meetings, partner-confidential summits — any event where the host explicitly bans sales framing or the brief signals Chatham House conditions.
- ❌ Does NOT apply to standard B2B conferences, trade shows, webinars, or events where lead generation is a stated objective. For those, "pipeline" / "MQL" / "SQL" are operational vocabulary and should be used precisely.

**Pre-submission scan (mandatory for closed-door events):** run a literal find-on-page check for every forbidden term in your draft output before submitting. If any are found, rewrite the passage and re-scan. The check is fast (under 30 seconds), and the cost of one slip is the entire credibility of the format.

### Cut / Keep at 4-6 weeks lead time

When lead time is compressed but feasible (4-6 weeks for a non-CxO audience, 8+ weeks for CxO), prioritise as follows:

**Keep (non-negotiable):**
- Guest curation (a wrong invite list ruins the dinner regardless of execution quality)
- Personal invitations from named host
- Venue and seating plan
- Moderator brief (3-5 prepared questions, conversation flow)
- Note-taking + 1-page insight brief delivered T+3 days

**Compress (reduce scope, don't skip):**
- Post-event materials: 1-page summary only, no recap video
- Survey: 3 questions max ("most valuable moment", "what should we discuss next", "would you attend again")
- Pre-event communication: 1 logistics email at T-1 week (no drip)

**Cut (skip entirely, no value at micro scale):**
- Branded signage and printed agendas (the venue knows you're coming)
- Photography (use phones for one host-with-each-guest snapshot)
- Pre-event social media (private events don't need a hashtag)
- RACI matrix (host + 1 organizer is the entire team)
- Sponsor activations (not the format)

### Top-3 Risks for Micro Events

For events of 10-50 attendees, focus the risk register on these three. Other risks from the standard taxonomy can be left to general operational discipline.

| Risk | Mitigation | Contingency |
|---|---|---|
| **Low confirmed attendance** (<60% of target) at T-1 week | Build a 1.5-3× invitation pipeline (see Acceptance Rate Expectations above). Phone follow-up at T-2 weeks for non-responders. | Activate a "shadow list" of 2-3 standby invitees with named-host personal asks at T-3 days. Don't downsize the venue (smaller venue + half-empty room looks worse than full venue + a few extra chairs). |
| **High-value guest no-show on event day** (the keynote conversation depends on this person) | Confirm attendance T-3 days with a personal text from the host, not the events team. Send venue + arrival logistics. | Reframe the conversation around an adjacent topic the moderator is prepared for. Have a backup question track. Don't apologise — pivot. |
| **Conversation falls flat** (moderator prompt doesn't land, guests are quiet) | Brief the moderator to over-prepare with 5-6 questions, but be willing to abandon any that don't land in the first 2 minutes. Plant a "first responder" (a known-vocal guest briefed to take the first question). | Switch to round-the-table prompting: "Let's go around — one sentence each on [topic]". Forces participation without exposing the moderator's read. |

## Advisory Board Meeting

**Format:** 8-20 members, structured feedback session, half-day to full-day.

### Key Differences from Other Events

- NDA required (sign before or at start — have copies ready)
- Compensation common (honorarium — typical range is 2.5-10× the per-person dinner cost — travel covered, or product credits)
- Agenda is collaborative (members help shape it, not just respond)
- Output is a formal document, not just notes

### Advisory Board Checklist

**Pre-meeting (T-4 weeks):**
- [ ] Confirm members and RSVP (phone, not email blast)
- [ ] Distribute NDA for signature
- [ ] Share pre-read materials (product roadmap, research, specific questions — 5-10 pages max)
- [ ] Confirm compensation/travel arrangements
- [ ] Send logistics details (venue, hotel, travel, agenda)

**Meeting structure (4-6 hours):**
| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0:00-0:15 | Welcome, NDA reminder, Chatham House Rules, agenda review |
| 0:15-0:45 | Company update / context setting (brief, not a sales pitch) |
| 0:45-1:30 | Topic 1: Structured feedback session (present → discuss → capture) |
| 1:30-1:45 | Break |
| 1:45-2:30 | Topic 2: Structured feedback session |
| 2:30-3:15 | Topic 3: Open discussion / emerging topics |
| 3:15-3:30 | Wrap-up: key takeaways, next steps, next meeting date |

**Post-meeting (T+1 week):**
- [ ] Thank-you email with summary of key insights
- [ ] Internal action item document (who does what based on feedback)
- [ ] Process compensation/expense reimbursement
- [ ] Schedule next meeting (quarterly or semi-annually)

## VIP Networking Reception

**Format:** 30-150 guests, standing format, 2-3 hours, catering-focused, no fixed program.

### Planning Essentials

- **Venue:** Distinctive location matters more than for conferences. Rooftop, gallery, restaurant private room. The venue IS the experience.
- **Timing:** Evening (18:00-21:00). Often parallel to a larger event (side event at a conference, pre-event reception).
- **Invitation:** Curated guest list. Mix of customers, prospects, partners, executives.
- **Program:** Minimal. 5-minute welcome from host, then open networking. Maybe one 3-minute "lightning insight" from a speaker. No panels, no slides.
- **Catering:** This IS the program. Quality over quantity. Standing format with food stations, not a sit-down dinner. Premium drinks.
- **Facilitation:** Host team actively introduces guests to each other. Prepare a cheat sheet: who is who, and who should meet whom.

## Common Elements

### Guest List Management (replaces Registration)

VIP events use guest lists, not registration systems.

| Field | Source | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Name | CRM or manual list | First + Last |
| Company + Title | CRM | For place cards and introductions |
| Relationship owner | Internal | Who invited this person? Who greets them? |
| Dietary / accessibility | Personal email ask | "Any dietary needs we should know about?" |
| RSVP status | Tracked manually | Invited → Confirmed → Declined → No Response |
| Plus-one | If applicable | Executive dinners: no. Receptions: sometimes. |

### Follow-Up (Critical Difference)

VIP event follow-up is relationship management, not lead gen.

- **T+1 day:** Personal thank-you from host. Not template. Reference something specific from the conversation.
- **T+1 week:** If any commitments were made ("I'll send you that report"), deliver now.
- **T+2 weeks:** Connect guests who should know each other but didn't meet at the event.
- **No automated email sequences.** No surveys. No "rate your experience 1-5." This is relationship territory.

### Budget Benchmarks

Use VIP Reception as the 1.0× baseline and scale by event type:

| Event Type | Cost Index | Includes |
|---|---|---|
| VIP Reception | 1.0× (baseline) | Venue, canapés, premium drinks, staff |
| Executive Dinner | 1.5-2.5× | Venue, 3-course dinner, wine, host gifts |
| Advisory Board | 2.0-3.0× + honorarium | Venue, catering, materials, travel, compensation |

Actual per-person costs vary by city and market. Zurich/Geneva: add 30-40% to local benchmarks. London: similar to Munich.
