## Webinar vs. Conference

| Dimension | Webinar | Conference |
|---|---|---|
| Lead time | 2-4 weeks | 3-12 months |
| Duration | 30-60 minutes | 1-3 days |
| Speakers | 1-3 | 10-50+ |
| Registration | Landing page, 3-4 fields max | Full platform, 5-7 fields |
| No-show rate | 40-60% (register 2x capacity) | 10-20% (paid), 30-50% (free) |
| Content afterlife | On-demand replay is often more valuable than live | Recordings are supplementary |
| Budget | 0.01-0.1× conference cost | 1.0× (baseline) |
| Venue | None — platform is the venue | Physical or hybrid |
| Follow-up split | Attendees vs. no-shows get different sequences | One sequence for all |

## 2-Week Webinar Timeline

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| D-14 | Topic confirmed, speaker confirmed, title and abstract finalized |
| D-13 | Registration landing page live. Email #1 to list (announcement + register CTA) |
| D-10 | Social post #1 (LinkedIn). Speaker shares with their network |
| D-7 | Email #2 (reminder, add speaker bio, teaser content). Social post #2 |
| D-5 | Dry run / rehearsal with speaker and moderator (30 min). Test all tech. |
| D-3 | Email #3 (final reminder, calendar add). Social post #3 |
| D-1 | Final reminder email to registered. Test platform login. |
| D-0 | Go live. Moderator opens 5 min early. Record from start. |
| D+1 | Thank-you email to attendees (with replay link + resources). Separate email to no-shows ("You missed it — watch the replay"). |
| D+3 | On-demand replay published. Social post with replay link. |
| D+7 | Follow-up email with related content / next webinar in series. |

## 4-Week Webinar Timeline

Same as 2-week but with more promotion time:

| Week | Focus |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | Topic, speaker, landing page, email #1 |
| Week 2 | Promotion push: email #2, social posts, partner sharing, paid ads if budget |
| Week 3 | Rehearsal, email #3, speaker network amplification, content teaser (blog post or short video) |
| Week 4 | Final reminders, go live, follow-up sequence |

## Platform Setup Checklist

Run through BEFORE the rehearsal, not on webinar day.

- [ ] Platform selected and account configured (Zoom Webinar, ON24, GoToWebinar, Teams Town Hall, Livestorm)
- [ ] Registration page connected (platform-native or external with redirect)
- [ ] Confirmation email customized (not platform default — include calendar link, speaker info)
- [ ] Reminder emails configured (24h and 1h before)
- [ ] Branding applied (logo, colors, background image)
- [ ] Presenter roles assigned (host, presenter, moderator — different permissions)
- [ ] Recording enabled and storage location confirmed
- [ ] Q&A and polls configured (live Q&A panel, pre-loaded poll questions)
- [ ] Practice session / green room scheduled
- [ ] Backup plan documented: if primary platform fails, backup Zoom link ready to share via email
- [ ] Post-webinar redirect configured (to replay page or resource download)
- [ ] Analytics/tracking connected (UTM parameters on registration link, CRM integration)

### Rehearsal Checklist (30 minutes, D-5 to D-3)

- [ ] Speaker audio and video quality verified
- [ ] Screen sharing tested with actual slides
- [ ] Backup slides uploaded to platform (in case of speaker tech failure)
- [ ] Moderator Q&A workflow tested (how questions flow from audience to moderator to speaker)
- [ ] Polls tested (appear correctly, results display works)
- [ ] Recording tested (start, stop, where it saves)
- [ ] Timing rehearsed: intro (2-3 min), content (25-40 min), Q&A (10-15 min), close (2 min)
- [ ] Emergency protocol confirmed: who takes over if speaker drops? Who communicates to audience?

## Webinar-Specific KPIs

| KPI | Benchmark | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Registration rate | Depends on list size and topic | Track registrations per email sent |
| Live attendance rate | 40-60% of registered | Higher for niche topics, lower for broad |
| Engagement rate | 30-50% interact (poll, Q&A, chat) | Measure active participants, not passive viewers |
| Average watch time | 70-85% of total duration | Drop-off after 45 min is steep |
| On-demand views (30 days) | 50-100% of live attendance | Often exceeds live — promote the replay |
| CTA conversion | 5-15% click post-webinar CTA | Depends on offer relevance |
| Pipeline generated | Track per webinar, 90-day window | Tag leads in CRM with webinar source |

**The real ROI metric for webinars is live + on-demand combined.** A webinar with 200 live attendees and 300 replay views reached 500 people at roughly 0.01-0.1× the per-person cost of an equivalent conference session.

## Webinar Series Planning

For recurring webinar programs (monthly, bi-weekly):

**Series structure:**
- 6-12 episodes per season/series
- Consistent day and time (e.g., every second Thursday at 11:00 CET)
- Overarching theme with individual episode topics
- Mix speaker types: internal experts, customers, external thought leaders, panels

**Series-specific considerations:**
- Serial registration (register once for all) vs. individual (register per episode) — serial has lower friction but lower per-episode engagement data
- Retention tracking: what % of Episode 1 registrants attend Episode 4?
- On-demand library: build a searchable archive of all past webinars — becomes a content asset
- Promotion cadence: announce full series at launch, then promote each episode individually 7-10 days before

**Content arc planning:**
| Episode | Purpose |
|---|---|
| 1 | Set the stage — broad topic, high-profile speaker, maximum promotion |
| 2-4 | Deep dives — specific subtopics, practical focus |
| 5 | Customer story / case study — social proof, change of pace |
| 6 | Wrap-up / panel — synthesize learnings, audience Q&A heavy |
