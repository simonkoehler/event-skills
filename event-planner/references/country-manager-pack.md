A packaged template for handing a single roadshow city to a regional country manager. Each per-city instance fills the placeholders below; the central team retains ownership of the cross-city sequence, brand, and consolidated analytics.

## 1. Scope: Central vs. Country Manager

A bright line. Without one, every city becomes a 1:1 negotiation between central and the country manager — which is how 6-city roadshows turn into 6 weeks of friction.

| Workstream | Central owns | Country manager owns |
|---|---|---|
| Master brand, key visual, deck template | ✅ | — |
| Speaker bench (cross-city pool) | ✅ | Local moderator selection from pool |
| Date sequencing across cities | ✅ | Confirms availability of local team |
| Venue criteria (this template's §4) | ✅ | Sources 2-3 venues, picks one, signs contract within criteria |
| Invitation copy template | ✅ | Localizes top 3 references + signs personally |
| ICP / target account list | Sets default ICP | Adds 50-100 local target accounts |
| Registration platform + consent text | ✅ | Validates local-language version with country lawyer |
| Catering vendor | — | ✅ |
| Local marketing channels (events.<country>, partner co-promo) | Approves channel list | Executes on local channels |
| Day-of execution | Sends central producer | Hosts, opens, handles escalation |
| Photo / video consent at door | ✅ (master script) | Briefs local staff |
| Post-event lead handover to local Sales | Owns export | Owns introductions + meeting follow-up |

**Conflict resolution rule:** If central and country manager disagree on a single-city decision, country manager wins on local norms (language, venue style, hospitality cap), central wins on brand and pricing. If the disagreement is on the rule itself, escalate to event lead, not to a back-and-forth email thread.

## 2. Invitation Copy — Three Language Variants

Each variant follows the same structure: **opener (personal) → why this is for them (1 sentence) → what they'll get (3 bullets) → logistics (1 line) → RSVP CTA → signature (named country manager, not "events team").**

### DE variant

```
Sehr geehrte/r {{first_name}} {{last_name}},

am {{event_date_local}} sind wir mit unserer Roadshow {{event_name}} in {{city}}. 
Ich lade Sie persönlich ein.

Warum für Sie: {{one_sentence_relevance_to_their_industry_or_role}}.

Was Sie mitnehmen:
- {{takeaway_1}}
- {{takeaway_2}}
- {{takeaway_3}}

{{venue_short}}, {{time_start}}–{{time_end}}. Kein Vortragsmarathon — 
Diskussion mit {{number}} Praktikern aus Ihrer Branche.

Antwortbitte bis {{rsvp_deadline}}: {{rsvp_link_or_email}}

Mit besten Grüßen
{{country_manager_name}}
{{country_manager_title}}, {{company_name}} {{country_entity_short}}
```

### EN variant (for IT, ES, LU, and English-language tracks in CH)

```
Hi {{first_name}},

We're bringing our {{event_name}} roadshow to {{city}} on {{event_date_local}}, 
and I wanted to extend a personal invitation.

Why this is for you: {{one_sentence_relevance}}.

What you'll take away:
- {{takeaway_1}}
- {{takeaway_2}}
- {{takeaway_3}}

{{venue_short}}, {{time_start}}–{{time_end}}. Not a presentation marathon — 
a working discussion with {{number}} practitioners in your industry.

RSVP by {{rsvp_deadline}}: {{rsvp_link_or_email}}

Best,
{{country_manager_name}}
{{country_manager_title}}, {{company_name}} {{country_entity_short}}
```

### IT variant

```

- {{takeaway_1}}

{{venue_short}}, dalle {{time_start}} alle {{time_end}}. Non un susseguirsi 
di presentazioni — una discussione operativa con {{number}} colleghi 
del Suo settore.

RSVP entro il {{rsvp_deadline}}: {{rsvp_link_or_email}}

Cordialmente,
{{country_manager_name}}
{{country_manager_title}}, {{company_name}} {{country_entity_short}}
```

### ES variant

```
Estimado/a {{first_name}} {{last_name}},

el {{event_date_local}} llevamos nuestro roadshow {{event_name}} a {{city}}, 
y quería invitarle personalmente.

Por qué es para usted: {{one_sentence_relevance}}.

Qué se llevará:
- {{takeaway_1}}
- {{takeaway_2}}
- {{takeaway_3}}

{{venue_short}}, de {{time_start}} a {{time_end}}. No es una sucesión 
de presentaciones — es una discusión operativa con {{number}} profesionales 
de su sector.

RSVP antes del {{rsvp_deadline}}: {{rsvp_link_or_email}}

Un cordial saludo,
{{country_manager_name}}
{{country_manager_title}}, {{company_name}} {{country_entity_short}}
```

**Formal address rule:** German `Sie`, Italian `Lei`, Spanish `usted` — never `du` / `tu` / `tú` in an invitation, even in markets where informal address is increasingly common in customer relationships. Switzerland: same as Germany. Country manager can switch to informal in follow-up if the recipient does first.

**Technical terms stay English** even in local-language variants (product names, platform features, feature flags, SKUs). Do not translate product names.

## 3. ICP One-Pager

Used by the country manager to add 50-100 local target accounts to the central list.

```
# ICP for {{event_name}} — {{city}} Edition

## Job titles in scope
- Tier 1 (decision-maker): {{title_t1_a}}, {{title_t1_b}}, {{title_t1_c}}
- Tier 2 (influencer): {{title_t2_a}}, {{title_t2_b}}
- Tier 3 (technical evaluator): {{title_t3_a}}

## Industries in scope
{{industry_1}}, {{industry_2}}, {{industry_3}}

## Company sizes (priority order)
1. Enterprise ({{>1000_employees}}) — strategic accounts
2. Mid-market ({{100-1000}}) — high-yield
3. SMB ({{<100}}) — only if multiple decision-makers attend

## Geography
- Primary catchment: {{city}} + {{nearest_metro_1}} + {{nearest_metro_2}}
- Secondary: nationwide if travel budget covers

## Exclusions (do not invite)
- Direct competitors of {{company_name}}
- Direct field teams of {{strategic_partner}} (handled by central with the partner)
- Existing customers already on the {{flagship_event_name}} invite list (avoid duplicate touch)
- Recipients of similar event invitations within the past 90 days (avoid invite fatigue)

## Quality bar
A country-manager-added account must have at least one of:
- Active project in the BU domain ({{Data & AI / Cloud / Security / etc.}})
- Prior touch with {{company_name}} (sales, marketing, support) in the past 12 months
- Named referral from an existing customer
```

## 4. Venue Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Capacity | 30-person seated theater OR 40-person reception (whichever the format calls for) |
| AV | HDMI + USB-C input, wireless lavalier mic, projector or 4K display, hardwired internet 100+ Mbps |
| Hybrid-readiness | Not required for roadshow stops — these are in-person only |
| Catering | Lunch + 2 coffee breaks OR reception canapés (whichever the format calls for) |
| Dietary accommodation | Vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, halal default; kosher on request |
| Accessibility | Wheelchair access, accessible WC, hearing-loop preferred |
| Location | Walking distance from a major train station OR < 20 min taxi from primary business district |
| Privacy | Private room with door (no shared event space, no walk-by traffic from other events) |
| Catering cancellation window | 72h or better — multi-city dates can slip |
| Country-manager fit | Country manager would walk in and recognize this as "where we host our customers" |

**Venue-type defaults by city:** Hotel meeting room (Frankfurt / Vienna / Zurich), restaurant private dining (Milan / Barcelona / Madrid), business club (Luxembourg). Country manager picks within the default unless they have a strong reason otherwise.

## 5. VAT / GDPR per-country quick-reference

| Country | VAT (standard) | Reverse charge for B2B intra-EU | Data protection regime | Lawful basis to default to |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Germany (DE) | 19% | Yes | GDPR + BDSG | Art. 6(1)(b) for registration, Art. 6(1)(a) for marketing follow-up |
| Austria (AT) | 20% | Yes | GDPR + DSG | Same as DE |
| Switzerland (CH) | 8.1% | No (CH outside EU) — Swiss VAT applies | revFADP (separate from GDPR) | revFADP Art. 31 transparency + explicit consent for marketing |
| Italy (IT) | 22% | Yes | GDPR + Codice Privacy + Garante guidance | Art. 6(1)(a) explicit consent default — Garante interprets legitimate interest narrowly for marketing |
| Spain (ES) | 21% | Yes | GDPR + LOPDGDD | Art. 6(1)(a) consent; double opt-in not required but recommended |
| Luxembourg (LU) | 17% | Yes | GDPR + CNPD guidance | Art. 6(1)(b) + (a); broadly similar to DE/AT |
| Poland (PL) | 23% | Yes | GDPR + Polish DP Act | Art. 6(1)(a); UODO supervisory authority |

**Switzerland-specific extras (Zurich, Geneva, Basel stops):**
- revFADP requires per-event Data Controller identity disclosure on registration page
- Swiss customs / ATA Carnet required for shipping swag, demo hardware, or printed materials across the CH border. Lead time 7-10 business days for ATA Carnet issuance.
- Swiss VAT charged on invoice; reverse charge does NOT apply for events in CH.
- Formal address: same `Sie`-form as Germany. Canton nuance only for printed materials (BE / GE — French; ZH / BS / SG — German).

**30-minute legal review per country** before invitations send. Country manager submits the localized invitation + consent text + privacy notice to the country-licensed lawyer (or CH-licensed for Swiss events) for a sign-off. Not a full review — a focused "does this comply with our country's specifics" check.

## 6. Day-of Run-Sheet Template

```
# Run-Sheet — {{event_name}}, {{city}}, {{date_local}}

## T-90 min — Setup
- {{time-90}}: Venue arrival, AV check, signage placement, name-tag table setup
- {{time-60}}: Catering arrives, country manager arrives, central producer arrives
- {{time-30}}: Registration desk ready, first attendee arrival window opens

## T-0 — Welcome
- {{time_start}}: Doors open, coffee/welcome drink
- {{time_start+15}}: Country manager opens (3-5 min), introduces format and one local context line

## T-0 to T-end — Program
- Block 1: {{topic_1}} — {{moderator}} ({{duration}})
- Coffee break ({{duration_break}})
- Block 2: {{topic_2}} — {{moderator}} ({{duration}})
- Discussion / Q&A ({{duration_discussion}})
- {{time_end}}: Country manager closes, names next steps, hands over to networking

## T+15 to T+90 min — Networking
- Reception OR seated lunch (per format)
- Country manager + central producer + speakers floor-walk for 1:1 conversations
- Photo opportunity for social if attendees consented at door

## T+end — Teardown
- {{time+end}}: Final guests depart, central producer collects materials
- Same-evening export of attendance log to central event-analytics

## Escalation contacts
- Country manager (primary on-site): {{name}} / {{mobile}}
- Central producer (logistics): {{name}} / {{mobile}}
- Venue contact (catering / AV / power): {{name}} / {{mobile}}
- Emergency (medical / fire): local emergency services + venue first-aid station
```

## 7. Post-event handover to Sales

Within 48h of the event close, the country manager hands the local Sales team:

1. **Attendee list export** with consent flags per attendee — central event-analytics produces, country manager forwards
2. **Top 5 conversations** notes the country manager took during the day (free text, not CRM-fed; for context only)
3. **Named introductions** the country manager promised on the day — owner + date promised + status
4. **Recording or no-recording note** depending on per-event consent posture
5. **Pre-flagship conversion tracker** — list of attendees who registered for {{flagship_event_name}} within 14 days post-event (this is the {{conversion_target_pct}} KPI from the roadshow brief)

Country manager does NOT update CRM with conversation content unless explicit consent was given. Names + company + attendance fact + consent flag is the boundary.

## 8. Country-Manager Do / Don't

**Do:**
- Personally sign every invitation. No "Events Team" sender.
- Phone follow-up at T-2 weeks for non-responders. Not email.
- Open the event yourself with 3-5 minutes of local context. Not central.
- Walk the floor at networking. Not the central producer.
- Handle dietary / accessibility requests by calling the venue, not by escalating to central.
- Localize the top 3 customer references in the invitation copy — replace central's defaults with regional customer names (with their permission).

**Don't:**
- Promise {{flagship_event_name}} discount or comp tickets without checking with central — pricing is centrally owned.
- Share attendee data with local partners or strategic-partner field teams without explicit consent.
- Move the date once invitations have gone out. Country managers can move dates only between locked-in milestones.
- Run a different format from other cities ("we do dinners not lunches in IT") without raising it in the cross-city sequencing call.
- Update CRM with conversation content. Names + attendance fact + consent flag only.
- Translate technical product names, platform features, or SKUs into local language.
